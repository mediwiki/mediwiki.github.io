General Characteristics

  1. PBC is a chronic and progressive cholestatic liver disease characterized by destruction of ONLY intrahepatic bile ducts with portal inflammation and scarring
    • Most common cause of vanishing bile duct syndrome (progressive ductopenia)
  2. It is a slowly progressive disease with a variable course. It may progress to cirrhosis and end-stage liver failure.
  3. It is an autoimmune disease that is often associated with other autoimmune disorders.
  4. It is most common in middle-aged women. (F>M 9:1, 30-65yo), may be predisposed by a positive family history

Pathophysiology

  • Inflammation and progressive destruction (?autoimmune) of small and medium-sized intrahepatic bile ducts (progressive ductopenia) → defective bile duct regeneration → chronic cholestasis → secondary hepatocyte damage (increased concentration of toxins that get excreted via bile) → gradual portal and periportal fibrotic change → liver failure → liver cirrhosis and portal hypertension (Advanced)

Clinical Features

  1. Fatigue
  2. Cholestatic disorder
    1. Pruritus (early in course of disease)
    2. Jaundice (late in course of disease)
  3. RUQ discomfort
  4. Xanthomata and xanthelasmata ← due to impaired metabolism and excretion of cholesterol
  5. Maldigestion (reduced fat soluble vitamin absorption) → Osteoporosis → Perform DEXA upon diagnosis
  6. Portal HTN (with resultant sequelae)

Diagnosis

  1. Laboratory findings
    • Cholestatic LFTs: elevated ALP, GGT, conjugated bilirubin
      • Elevated cholesterol, HDL
    • Positive antimitochondrial antibodies (AMAs) found in 90% to 95% of patients. This is the hallmark of the disease (specificity of 98%). If serum is positive for AMAs, perform a liver biopsy to confirm diagnosis
      • Positive ANA in up to 70%
      • Elevated immunoglobulin M
  2. Liver biopsy (percutaneous or laparoscopic) to confirm the diagnosis.
  3. Abdominal ultrasound or CT scan to rule out biliary obstruction.

Treatment

  1. Treatment is symptomatic for pruritus (cholestyramine) and osteoporosis (calcium, bisphosphonates, vitamin D).
  2. Medical: Ursodeoxycholic acid (a hydrophilic bile acid) has been shown to slow progression of the disease, delays transplantation need, and is hepatoprotective
  3. Liver transplantation is the only curative treatment available.

Complications

  • Malabsorption → ?osteoporosis
  • Liver cirrhosis → portal hypertension
