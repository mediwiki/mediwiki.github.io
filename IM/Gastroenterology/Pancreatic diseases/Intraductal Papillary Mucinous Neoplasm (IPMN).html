Intraductal Papillary Mucinous Neoplasm (IPMN)

Background

  • Nil gender predisposition
  • 60-80 year old

Classifications

  1. Main duct

Main duct IPMN: • >10 mm diameter at least 50% chance of cancer • Resect all involved pancreas when possible • May require a total pancreatectomy • 5-10mm (worrisome but not absolute criteria) • At ERCP: Patulous ampulla with mucous flow

  1. Side branch (branch-type)

Often multiple small cysts : Head of pancreas common – Frequency increases with age – Typically picked up incidentally – Often can see a communication with a side duct of the pancreatic duct – Epithelial change with varying degrees of dysplasia leading to mucous production, all the way to high grade dysplasia and carcinoma insitu. – Some become Mucinous Cystic Carcinoma. – An example of an adenoma/carcinoma sequence.

Side branch: lower chance of cancer than main – International Guidelines: – Resect when 1 or more worrying feature: • Size >3cm (worrisome, not absolute criteria) • Symptomatic (usually pain or pancreatitis) • Mural nodule (esp. with enhancement) • Concerning features on EUS and aspiration (High grade dysplasia) – Dilemma is that SB-IPMN occur in elderly and usually require a pancreatico- duodenectomy (Big Time Surgery) for a condition with a modest malignant potential

  1. Mixed
