Prinzmetal Angina

Background

  • Involves transient coronary vasospasm that usually is accompanied by a fixed atherosclerotic lesion but can also occur in normal coronary arteries.
  • Episodes of angina occur at rest and are associated with ventricular arrhythmias, some of which may be life threatening. The angina classically occurs at night.
    • Hallmark is transient ST-segment elevation (not depression) on ECG during chest pain, which represents transmural ischemia.
  • Taken from history, if the patient was recently using cocaine, they are at high risk for having Prinzmetal angina

Clinical features

Diagnostic criteria

  • Coronary angiography is a definitive test—displays coronary vasospasm when the patient is given IV ergonovine or acetylcholine (to provoke vasoconstriction).
    • ergonovine is Alpha-adrenergic agonist (+ DA + 5-HT agonist)
    • Vasodilators—CCBs and nitrates have been proven to be helpful.
    • Risk factor modification including smoking cessation and lipid lowering is also indicated where appropriate.

Investigations

Management

  • Calcium channel blockers (both DHP and non-DHP) and nitrates are used in Prinzmetal angina
  • RF modification - smoking cessation, lipid lowering, stop cocaine use
