Raynaud's Phenomena

Background

Pathology

  1. Primary Raynaud's
    • common in young women (<30y)
    • often have family history
    • ANA usually negative (note population positive ANA is pretty high, so can be overlap)
  2. Secondary Raynaud's
    • Onset in age > 35
    • More severe - Digital ulcers, pitting scars in fingers
    • Abnormal capillary microscopy
    • Presenece of autoantibodies - need to look
    • Associated with systemic illness including autoimmune disease (e.g. Scleroderma)
    • Associated with
      • Connective tissue disease: Scleroderma, Systemic Lupus Erythematosis, Mixed connective tissue disease (MCTD), Undifferentiated CTD, Sjogrens syndrome, Dermatomyositis
      • Occlusive arterial disease: [[Atherosclerosis]], Antiphospholipid syndrome (APLS), Thromboangiitis obliterans (Buerger's disease)
      • Vascular injury: Frostbite, vibratory trauma, Vinyl chloride, bleomycin, amphetamine, cocaine, Hyperviscosity/cold-reacting proteins
