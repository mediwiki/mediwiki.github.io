Vasculitis

When to consider vasculitis

  • Multisystem disease
  • unexplained constitutional ssx
  • skin lesions (palpable purpura)
  • Ischemic vascular changes (gangrene, claudication, Raynaud's, livedo)
  • Glomerulonephritis - RCC casts
  • mononeuritis multiplex
  • Myalgia, arthralgia/arthritis
  • Abdominal (intestinal angina) or testicular pain

Small vessels (Venules, Arterioles)

  • Drug induced vasculitis and serum sickness
    • PTU
    • Allupurinol
    • Levamisole tainted cocaine
  • IgA vasculitis (Henoch-Schonlein purpura)
  • Cryoglobulinaemia
  • Vasculitis associated with
    • Systemic Rheumatology diseases
    • Malignancy
    • Infections
  • Hypocomplementemic urticarial vasculitis
  • Goodpastures

Small and medium muscular arteries

Really rare diseases; PAN is prototypical

  • Classic Polyarteritis Nodosa (PAN)
  • ANCA associated vasculitis - morbidity due to renal failure or pulmonary hemorrhage/failure
    • C-ANCA (PR3) positive - higher mortality
      • Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (Wegeners Granulomatosis)
    • P-ANCA (MPO) positive
      • Microscopic Polyangiitis
      • Eosinophilic Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (Churg Strauss)
      • 10% of Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (Wegeners Granulomatosis) are MPO positive
      • High MPO titres can indicate Drug induced vasculitis
  • Kawasaki syndrome
  • Rheumatoid vasculitis
  • Systemic Lupus Erythematosis
  • Thromboangiitis obliterans (Buerger's disease)
  • Primary CNS vasculitis
  • Cogan's syndrome

Large arteries

Affects big blood vessels, such as temporal, retinal arteries, aorta, subclavian

  • Giant Cell Arteritis
  • Takayasu Arteritis

Any or all types of blood vessels

  • Behcets disease

Empirical treatment of vasculitis of unclear cause

  • Rituximab effective in ANCA vasculitis and noninferior to CYC
  • Plasmapheresis in aggressive ANCA disease - improves renal function

Conditions which mimic vasculitis

  • Atheroembolic disease - atherosclerotic emboli showering
  • infective endocarditis
  • cardiac myxoma
  • thrombotic disorders
    • Antiphospholipid syndrome (APLS)
    • Thrombotic Thrombocytopenic purpua
  • Drug-induced vascular damage
    • Ergot derivatives
    • Cocaine
    • Amphetamines

Finger tip ischemia approach

  • Raynauds/acral necrosis
  • antiphospholipid antibody syndrome
  • small vessel vasculitis (including RA)
  • Endocarditis
  • Medium vessel vasculitis (e.g. PAN)

Causes of pulmonary-renal syndrome - coughing up blood and blood in urine

  • Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (Wegeners Granulomatosis)
  • Systemic Lupus Erythematosis
  • Anti-glomerular Basement Membrane Antibody disease (Goodpasture syndrome)

Causes of necrotic lesions

  • Polyarteritis Nodosa (PAN)
  • Cryoglobulinaemia
  • Drug induced vasculitis - Cocaine related (typically skin disease)
    • resolves with resolution of contaminated cocaine

- antiSm - SLE - AntiGBM - Goodpasteurs - p-ANCA MPO - EGPA + MPO - p-ANCA PR3 - GPA Wegeners

- IV corticosteroids if abdo pain present
