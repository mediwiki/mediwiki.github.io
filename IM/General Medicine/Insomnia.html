Insomnia

Background

  • Complex health problem manifesting as
    • poor sleep quality
    • frustration with sleep quantity
    • difficulty initiating sleep
    • inability to return to sleep after awakening
  • Epidemiology
    • Prevlance increases with age
    • F > M affected
  • Causes
    • Medical disorders - cardiac, neurodegenerative, psychiastric
    • Medications - caffeine, alcohol, glucocorticoids, diuretics, antidepressants

Pathology

Clinical features

Diagnostic criteria

  • Chronic insomnia are symptoms which:
    1. Cause substantial functiona distress or impairment
    2. Occur > 3 nights/week for > 3 months
    3. Not associated with other sleep, medical or mental disorders
  • Diagnostic criteria for chronic insomnia is met by ~ 10% of adults

Investigations

  • History
    • Drug history - OTC medications, herbal supplements, caffeine, alcohol tobacco, illicit drugs
    • sleep pattern
      • initiation
      • maintenance
      • quality
    • Environmental factors
      • work schedule
      • electronic use - screen time, devices
    • Collateral history from partner
  • Physical examination
    • Sleep apnoea
    • Restless legs syndrome
  • Athens insomnia scale and Insomnia severity index
  • Investigations
    • Polysomnography if concern of sleep apnoea, narcolepsy or sleep movement disorder (NOT first line)

Management

  • Institute a sleep diary
  • CBT for insomnia (CBT-I) - First line
    • Cognitive therapy - address maladaptive beliefs and expectations
    • Educational interventions - sleep hygiene
      • Effectiveness of blue light filtering has not been established
      • Sleep hygiene education not shown to be as effective as standaline therapy
      • During the day
        • Ensure adequate natural light exposure
        • Avoid napping
        • Avoid the following close to bedtime:
          • Substances which can fragment/disturb sleep (caffeine, nicotine, alcohol)
          • Vigorous exercise
          • Large meals
          • Emotionally upsetting activities
      • At bedtime
        • Establish regular bedtime routine - ~ 30 minutes prior
        • associate bed and bedroom for sleep
        • Dark, quiet environment
        • Stable bedtime and arising time
        • Do not spend more than 8 hours in bed, 20 minutes awake in bed
        • Avoid screen time
    • Behavioural interventions - sleep restriction therapy, stimulus control therapy, relaxation techniques
    • Brief behavioural therapy is abbreviated CBT-I, focusing on behavioural and educational interventions only
  • Pharmacologic therapy
    • Associated with harms - daytime drowsiness, increased risk of falls/hip fractures, medication related hallucinations
    • should only be used in short-term trials (~ 4-5 weeks) and target the problematic phases (onset vs. maintenance)
    • minimal evidence to support long temr use on function and QoL
    • Options
      • Benzodiazepines
      • Nonbenzodiazepine GABA-receptor agonist (Z-drugs)
      • Melatonin receptor agonist
      • OTC medications
        • Anticholinergics
        • Antihistamines
