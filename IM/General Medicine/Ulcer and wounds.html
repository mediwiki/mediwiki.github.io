Ulcer and wounds

Background

Pathology

Clinical features

  • History
    • Previous ulcers, surgical history
    • smoking status and alcohol history
    • wound concerns - pain, restricted mobility, swelling, exudate leakage, odour
    • wound onset and duration
    • wound location and management options (E.g. orthotics and footware)
    • wound progress (e.g. is it healing)
    • treatment attempts
  • Past medical history
    • Limitations to skin circulation/oxygenation - chronic venous insufficiency, peripheral arterial disease, heart failure, impaired respiratory function, blood disorders (anemia, polycythemia, thrombocytemia)
    • Altered immune function - rheumatoid arthritis, systemic lupus erythematosus, inflammatory bowel disease, HIV
    • Impaired healing - endocrine or metabolic disorders (eg diabetes mellitus, thyroid dysfunction, estrogen deficiency), disseminated malignancy, peripheral oedema, disorders affecting nutrition (eg malabsorption)
    • Mobility/self care - poor vision, reduced hand function or range of movement, neurological conditions, arthritis, obesity, dementia or other cognitive or developmental disorder, major psychiatric disorders, incontinence.
  • Physical exam
    • Skin integrity - dryness, purpura, atrophy, solar exposure and skin cancer, dermatitis, allergy to topical products (including dressings, neuropathy, tinea
    • vascular disease (chronic venous insufficiency, peripheral arterial disease, mixed vascular disease)
    • heart and lung disease (eg breathlessness on exertion, cyanosis, signs of right-sided heart failure
    • anaemia (eg pallor)
    • systemic infection (eg fever, tachycardia)
    • lymphadenopathy
    • peripheral oedema or lymphoedema
    • nutritional deficiency
    • dehydration
    • neurological disease (eg gait disturbance that influences calf muscle pump function, impaired upper limb function, sensor or motor impairment)
    • arthritis

Describing wounds

  • Tissue
    • devitalised tissue (necrosis, eschar, slough)
    • granulation tissue / hypergranulation / agranulation
    • epithelium
    • deeper tissues (bone, tendon, muscle, subcut fat)
    • foreign material/debris
  • Infection/inflammation
    • superficial infection - delayed healing, pain, exudate (esp if green), odour, abnormal granulation tissue
    • deep infection - increased size, temperature, bone probe, oedema, erythema > 2cm from wound margin, 'satellite' breakdown, yellow/green exudate
  • Moisture
    • quantity (none, scant, small, moderate, heavy)
    • type (serous, purulent)
  • Edge
    • flat, indistinct, sloping, punched out, undermined (not attached), hyperplastic (Heaped up), hyperkeratotic (callused skin at edge), rolled/curled under

Investigations

  • Blood tests (not required unless unclear etiology or delayed healing)
    • FBC, EUC, CMP, LFT, CRP
    • ESR ANA
    • TSH, Iron studies, VB12, folate, VitD
    • HbA1c, OGTT
  • Wound swabs should only be used when clinical suspicion of invasive infection
