Essential Pain Management

  • Pain can be acute or chronic

    - childbirth, trauma, cancer as causes of pain

Think about 1. Recognising - relationship between psychological and physical factors of pain - Definition - An unpleasant sensory and emotional experience associated with, or resembling that associated with, actual or potential tissue damage. (International Assocation for the Study of Pain) - affected by biopsychosocial factors - experience of pain should be respected - adverse effects on functional, social and psychological wellbeing 1. Assessing 2. Treatment - non-pharmacological strategies - RICE - for acute painful injuries - acupuncture - needles, heat and pressure to body; may be supplement to acute pain - TENS - mild impulses to help alter sensory signals; can manage acute short term pain - massage - can relax painful muscles, relieve stress/anxiety - cold/hot packs - ice for acute injuries pain/inflammation/swelling; heat for muscle pain/stiffness - no evidence for either - as per the patient's preference - movement - surgery - e.g. abscess drainage or appendicitis - physiotherapy - movement, activity and exercise - psychological strategies - education on what pain is and what is causing it - reassurance - self regulation and relaxation therapies to consider - pharmacological options - WHO pain ladder - reverse ladder used to step down pain - simple analgesia - paracetamol q6hrly - ibuprofen tds max - use lowest dose for shortest amount of time - opioids - tramadol - atypical opioid; has some role in neuropathic pain - morphine - can cause dependence/addiction if used for several weeks - pethidine - not typically used; can cause 'twitching' or 'fitting' from metabolite norpethidine - not recommended for cancer pain unless no stronger medication - other medications - Anti-neuropathic medications - amitriptyline - 5-10mg nocte starting dose - takes 4-10 days to work - modulation at the level of the dorsal horn - gabapentin - 100mg BD starting dose - SNRIs - venlafaxine, duloxetine - Local anaesthetics - lidocaine, bupivicaine - Other - ketamine - anaesthetic agent; low dose as analgesia - Nitrous Oxide - placebo effect - can sometimes work, effect is true, but cannot lie to patients as unethical

Opioid safety

  • addiction - Craving, loss of Control, negative Consequences
    • rare in severe acute nociceptive pain
  • respiratory depression - adequate monitoring can minimise

Cancer pain

  • Opioids reasonable breakthrough dose is 10-15% of the patient's daily dose, and then offering it as IR q1-2hrly
    • after 24h, can calculate total extra dose and then readjust the SR dose
