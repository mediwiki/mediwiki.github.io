Pharmacological options for pain management

Non-opioid therapy options

  • First line
    • Paracetamol
    • NSAIDs
      • Topical NSAIDs (e.g. diclofenac) have few systemic side effects
    • Muscle relaxants - short course may be considered for acute pain
  • Nonopioid adjuvants for neuropathic pain syndromes
    • Gabapentanoids (gabapentin, pregabain)
    • Serotonin-Norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors
    • Antiepileptic drugs (AEDs) including carbamazepine - appears to be effective in treatment of trigeminal neuralgia
    • Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs)
    • Medical cannabis

Opioid therapy options

World Health Organisation analgesic ladder

By the clock, by the mouth, by the ladder

  1. By the clock
    • Aim to give analgesia as scheduled rather than PRN medications
  2. By the mouth
    • Oral route preferred for ease of use
    • Use the least invasive routes (sublingual, subcutaneous) before using intravenous
    • NEVER use intramuscular routes
  3. By the ladder
    • Escalate drugs in the following order
      • Non-opioids (e.g. paracetamol, NSAIDs)
      • As necessary - mild opioids (E.g. codeine):
      • Strong opioids - e.g. morphine, hydromorphine
    • Adjuvants may be considered at ANY point of the ladder
      • Antidepressants (Amitriptyline)
      • Anticonvulsants (e.g. gabapentin)
      • Corticosteroids (E.g. dexamethasone)
      • Anxiolytics (E.g. diazepam)
