Nonpharmacological options for pain management

Options

  1. Distraction techniques - benefit for patients in ED and postoperative settings
    • Music therapy
  2. Structured physical therapy program for chronic pain
    • high quality evidence that PT improves pain and function
    • Continuation > 12 weeks based on clinical assessment and documented improvement
  3. Cognitive behavioural techniques
    • CBT, mindfulness, biofeedback - associated with reduced pain and improved overall function and mood
      • CBT to treat depression may lead to substantial improvements in chronic pain and depressive symptoms
  4. Complimentary and integrative therapies
    • Spinal manipulation, massage, acupuncture in chronic pain management - low to moderate quality evidence in support
  5. Interventional therapies
    • Anatomically targeted injection based therapies (E.g. nerve blocks, trigger point injections)
    • High frequency neurostimulation - holds promise for chronic pain
