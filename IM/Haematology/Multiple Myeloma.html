  • generalised osteopenia is commonly seen in MM - not lytic bone lesions
  • don't do CT with IV contrast even wth normal renal function as can precipitation renal failure
  • IgA paraprotiens have higher serum viscosity than IgG paraproteins
  • fever is worrysome features in MM

Epidemiology

  • mAge 69, more common in AA/pacific isalnders
  • Risks
    • radiation
    • toxic exposures
    • MGUS - 1% a year transition to MM

Diagnostic criteria

  1. monoclonal plasma cells in bone marrow and/orpresence of biopsy-proven plasmacytoma
  2. myeloma-related organ dysfunction (1+ of):
    • C: serum Calcium > 10.5mg/L or ULN
    • R: Renal insufficiency
    • A: Anaemia (Hb < 10g/dL)
    • B: Lytic bone lesions or osteoporosis

Investigations

  • Bloods: FBC, EUC, CMP
    • EPG IEPG SFLC
    • beta2 microglobulin
  • Urine: 24h protein electropheresis, immunofixation
  • Other:
    • skeletal survey
    • BMB/aspirate with cytogenetics
    • PET in selected situations

Staging - international staging system

Complications

  • bone disease/hypercalcaemia/fracture
  • Hyperviscosity - IgM, IgA, IgG3
  • recurrent infections - do not make normal IgG
  • renal failure - do not give IV contrast
    • hypercaecaelmia
    • myeloma kidney w/ light chains
    • hyperuricaemia
    • IV urography
    • dehydration
    • plasma cell infiltration
    • pyelonephritis
    • amyloidosis in lamda light chain restricted paraproteins
  • cardiac failure - amyloid, hyperviscosity, anaemia
  • anaemia - BM tumours (infiltration), renal dysfunction, myelosuppression, low endogenous EPO
  • Neuropathy - sensory +/- motor, amyloid, anti-myelin Ab

Management

  • Non-transplant
    • dexamethasone/bortezomib/cyclophosphamide
    • D/B/lenalidomide
    • D/lenalidomide
  • transplant - autologous SCT
    • 3 drug combination: Dexamethasone/bortezomib +
      • lenalidomide/cyclophosphamide/doxorubicin
    • autologous SCT - esp for younger patients, increased survival benefit
  • Bisphosphonates for bony disease
