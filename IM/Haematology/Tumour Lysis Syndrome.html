Case 7 - 53M Burkitt's lymphoma beginning induction chemotherapy. Started on allopurinol and hydration. 4/7 later, SCr 5.4 (1.0 b/l). UO = 20mM over last 24/24. Labs: K+ 6.0, Ca 6.1, PO4 8.3, Uric acid 15. Complains of muscle cramps and paraesthesias. - Next step - dialysis

Tumour lysis syndrome - Occurs in - large tumour burden with rapid cell kill (both needed) - most common in aggressive leukemias and lymphomas (e.g. Burkitt's) - uncommon with solid tumours - however dont get rapid enough cell kill for TLS - risk highest during induction chemo due to large tumour burden - fatal complications result in arrhythmias and renal failure - oliguria is a poor prognostic sign - Definition - K+ > 6.0 - Urine acid > 8 - serum phosphate >= 4.5 - Ca < 7 - Prophylaxis is key - aggressive hydration to maintain UO - rasburicase vs. allopurinol - Treatment of TLS - Rasburicase - recombinant urate oxidase - convers uric acid to allantoin - more soluble in urine - NOTE it is contraindicated in G6PD deficiency - if OLIGURIC or persistent metabolic abnormalities or severe symptoms â†’ dialysis needed - obviously cannot aggressively hydrate if cannot excrete urine
