Chronic lymphocytic leukemia

  • Epidemiology
    • heterogenous
    • most common leukemia in western world
    • median age 65 (older)
    • BM, LN, spleen involvement at diagnosis
    • B-cell malignancy - CD19, CD20, CD5, CD23
  • complications
    • AIHA
    • hypogammaglobulinaemia
    • secondary malignancy risk (defective immune surveillance)
  • Prognostic factors -IPI
    • TP53 (17p) del/mutation → strong determinant; worse in mutations
    • IGHV (unmutated)
    • B2 microglobulin > 3.5
    • clinical stage - Binet B/C
    • Age > 65
  • Who and when to treat - IPI score
    • low score → do not treat → 93% 5yr survival
    • intermediate (2-3) - no treat unless sx (79%)
    • high risk (4-6) → treat unless asx (63%)
    • very high esp tp53 (7-10) → novel inhibitor or clinical trial (23)
    • no increased survival with early CLL treatment
  • Treatment options
    • under 65 - ibrutinib PO
      • FCR if healthy, brutixumab/rituxin reasinable alternative
      • del17p - treatment naive; iburitinb; role of allogenic stem cell transplant unclear
      • unmutated IGHV - consider ibrutib or FCR
    • over 65
      • high risk cytogenetics - ibrutinib
      • others: bendamustine/rutix; CLB + obinutuzumab, CLB + ofatumumab; ibrutinib
  • complications
    • hypogammaglobulinaemia common
      • encapsulated organism infection
      • consider IVIg replacement for recurrent infections - pneumonia, bronchitis, sinusitis
    • progressive granulocytopenia - consider G-CSF
    • severe T-cell immunosuppression
      • listeria, PCP infections
      • fludarabine reduces CD4+ T-cell counts
      • PCP prophylaxis need
      • cMV reactivation in alemtuzumab (not used as much now)
      • funal infections (e.g. cryptococcus) seen in ibrutinib treated patients - in first several months
    • autoimmune complications
      • AIHA - treat with steroids, IVIg - seen after fludarabine Tx
      • ITP - steroids, IVIg
    • secondary malignancies - routine monitoring needed
    • risk of transformation to aggressive disease
      • rapidly LN enlargement, LDH increase, B-symptoms

Fludarabine - purine analogie - good response when combined; found in FCR (fludarabine, cyclophosphamide, rituxan) - SE - myelosuppression - TLS - hemolytic anaemia - unclear if related to treatment - opportunistic infecitons - due to T-cell suppression - shouldn't be combined with prednisone - need to given PCP prophylaxis

Ibrutinib - major agent - BTK inhibitor - oral administration - lymphocyte counts rise when therapy initiated - do not discontinue if an initial rise is seen - SE - myelosuppression - infections - esp fungal - atrial fibrillation (6%) - especially in older patients - bleeding (6%) - increased spontaneous bleed and post-op bleeding; particularly concerning for older patients - prior to MINOR surgery, hold for 3-5 days - for MAJOR surgery, hold for 7-10 days and a few days POST op - Studies - increases overall/progression free surivavl in frontline CLL (when combared to chlorambucil) - 90% 18m PFS vs 52% - 24m OS -98% vs. 85% chlorambucil

Venetoclax - upfront treatment of 17p abnormality CLL or second line after ibrutinib failure

Acute lymphocytic leukemia

  • Epidemiology
    • most common childhood cancer, age 3-5 years, bimodal peak at 50
    • assoc w/ Down syndrome, Bloom's, Ataxia-telangiectasia
    • ~ 15% of adult leukema
  • Prognostication
    • Age >= 350-40
    • features - WCC > 25000, ETP, T-ALL (genetic features)
    • cytogeneics - Ph+ (9, 22) or Ph-like (4;11) → seen in 25% of adults (>40% pts over 60)
    • delayed response to induction - > 4-6 weeks to get to remission
    • minimal residual disease - defined milestones
  • Treatment
    • induction - multiagent chemotherapy - e.g. cytoxan, adriamycin, L-aspariginase, vincristine, prednisone
    • consolidation - CNS prophylaxis (high relase risk), maintenance chemotherapy extends for ~ 2 years
    • Risk adapted therapy
      • high risk patients (cytogeneic abnormalities), adverse features (MRD)
        • should get treatment → allogeneic BMT in first remission
      • standard risk
        • chemotherapy alone + monitoring
      • relapse after chemo
        • salvage chemo → BMT, CAR-T cells
  • Subclasses of ALL
    • PH+ ALL
      • 25% adults, 40-60% of patients over 60
      • Diagnosis - FISH or cytogenetics; can be monitored with PCR
      • 9;22 Bcr-Abl translocation; p190 transcript 70%, p210 in 30% (CML)
      • treatment - CTx + TKI
        • TKI + steroids (i.e. no CTx) can be considered - italian data
        • Stem cell transplant if appropriate donor
        • PCR negative - TKI alone MAY be appropriate in younger adults
  • Prognosis
    • 5-year leukemia free survival 30-55%
  • Complications
    • infections - prednisone increases infection risk e.g. PCP - consider PCP prophlaxis
    • avascular necrosis - related to prior steroid therapy, esp in younger
    • relapse sites - CNS and testicles are potential sites of relapse

CAR T-cells - in ALL - attacks target/cosignalling domains

Myelodysplastic syndromes

  • stem cell disorder in older people
  • increased risk from previous ctx/rtx
  • cyotpenia on presentation

BMB - hypercellular marrow (MORE), trilinear dysplasia - irregular nuclear formations and RBC precursors

  • Differentials
    • Anaemia of chronic disease
    • marrow infiltration
    • drug toxicity (EtOH, chemotherapy, INH)
    • Viral infections (HIV)
    • Vitamin deficiency (B12, folate)
    • MCV often elevated in MDS patients
  • Prognostication - IPSS-R
    • cytogenetics - TP53, EZH2, ETV6, RUNX1, ASXL1
    • marrow blas popualtion
    • anaemia(Hb)
    • ANC low
    • Plt low
  • treatments - depends on age, performance, IPSS
    • supprtive care - anaemia only
      • chelation therapy for Fe overload
    • RARS - may respond to pyridoxine
    • growth factors
      • EPO (for EPO < 500), anaemia mx
      • G-CSF but can drive leukemic progression
    • CTx
      • 5-azacytidine (vs. conventional care, better overall survival)
      • lenalidomide (5q- syndrome)
        • older women, anaemia and high plt, SF3B1 mutation - prolonged course
    • allogenic BMT is only curative treatment

AML

  • cindience 2.4/100000
  • median age 65-70; assocated with prior irradiation and toxin exposure
    • may arise from previous MDS
  • BM findings - large blasts, auer rods, visible nucleoli; CD13, CD33, CD11
  • cytogeneics
    • favourable: t(8, 21), inv16 - AML1-core binding factor b
      • t(15,17)
    • adverse outcomes: -5, -7, 11q23, trisomy 8, 13, t(6;9)
      • complex - >=3 cytogenetics
    • cytogenetics most important in prognosis
  • key prognostic data
    • age
    • cytogenetics
    • rpimary vs. secondary (?from previous MDS/chemortx)
    • molecular studies
      • unfavourable: FLT3 ITD, KIT
      • favourabe: NPM1, CEBPA biallelic
  • treatment
    • inducation - anthracycline, Ara-C - complete remission 6-80%, esp younger pts
      • toxicity higher in older adults
      • lower intensity and recommended for older adults + p53 mutations (less chemo response anyway)
    • consolidation tx - chemotx/BMT
      • high dose ara-C most common
      • RCT no sng diff b/w consolidation ctx, allgenic BMT, autologous BMT
    • risk adapted tx
      • favourabe cytogenetics - chemotx alone
      • unfavourable cytogenetics - allogeneic BMT
      • intermediate risk - noraml cytogenetics - chemo OR BMT (but BMT favourited in Meta-Analysis)
  • complications
    • hyperleukocytosis - high blast count - affected respiratory compromise and/or AMS
      • medical emergency
      • Tx: IV hydration, rapid induction cTx, leukopheresis, radiation therapy

APML-M3

  • ATRA used to treat - improved complete remission, reduces DIC complications
  • arsenic trioxide to treat relapsed patients
  • ATRA+Arsenic associated with >85% Complete remission (i.e. nonchemotherapeutic regimen)

CML

  • myeloproliferative disease characterised by t(9;22)
  • med age 53, m=f, assoc w/ irradiation; med surv 5y
  • rpresentation
    • 50% asymptomatic; fatige, weight loss, abdo discomfort, easy brusiing
    • leukostasis, priapism, thrombosis are rare
  • Ph+ chromosome =in 90%; 5% have variant; 5% pH negative
  • treatments
    • TKI imatinib - BCR-ABl
      • oral, w/ minimal side effects - nausea (55), myalgia (49), edema (60), rash (32), diarrhoea (29)
    • chronic phase / low risk /intedmediate
      • imatinib or 2nd gen TKI (intermediate)
      • 95% 5yr survival
      • clinical milestones defined by PCR log reduction
      • can potentially stop TKI in some - 50% will recur, but can just restart
    • accelerated blast crisis
      • evaluate for BMT
      • CTx + TKI for blast crisis for these
