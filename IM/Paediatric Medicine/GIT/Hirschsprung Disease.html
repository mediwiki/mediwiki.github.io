Hirschsprung Disease aka Congenital Aganglionic Megacolon

absence of the myenteric and submucosal plexuses in continuity from the anus (rectum always affected) to a variable distance proximally (ENS migrate from proximal to distal – varying transition circumference)

  • Incidence 1:5000
  • Aetiology unknown – trisomy 21, MEN type 2, congenital central hypoventilation, neurofibromatosis
  • 50-90% present in the neonatal period – failure to pass meconium in 1st 48h, abdominal distension, feeding intolerance, bilious vomiting
  • Older PC: constipation, FTT, abdo pain/distension – in trisomy 21 may have >1y if previously fine
  • Lack of peristalsis  colonic obstruction
  • Ix
    • AXR: distended loops of bowel
    • Barium enema: dilated proximal colon with transition zone – where distension begins – resect
    • Dx <3 months: suction rectal biopsy  absence of ganglion cells and hypertrophied nerve trunks
    • >3 months: full thickness open biopsy
  • Complications: enterocolitis (fever and diarrhoea – toxic megacolon), perforation
  • Calretinin staining – negative in Hirschsprung’s
  • Surgical management – remove aganglionic bowel and bring normally innervated bowel to the anus whilst preserving sphincter function – usually 5cm below transition zone – check histology for ganglion cells all around
    • Swenson, Soave and Duhamel procedures – dentate line must be preserved for continence to be achieved
  • Rarely whole bowel is aganglionic  long term TPN
