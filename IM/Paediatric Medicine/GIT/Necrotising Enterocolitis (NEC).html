Necrotising enterocolitis (NEC):

  • neonatal bowel: multifactorial exaggerated inflammatory response due to an initiating event (e.g. feed) in the context of an immature intestine and immune system
  • 1:1000 live births, 90% preterm – gestational age main prognostic predictor
  • Pathophysiology – epithelial barrier disturbed, bacterial translocation
  • Severity  modified Bell’s Classification system
    • Stage 1: apnoea, bradycardia, temp instability, occult blood, mild abdo distension - distended, shiny abdomen
    • Progresses to shock, metabolic acidosis – perforation especially if NSAID
  • Ix
    • AXR: gaseous distension of bowel (luminal), portal venous gas, bowel perforation  pneumoperitoneum
    • Laboratory: ↑WCC, platelets, CRP
  • Mgmt
    • Non-operative (bowel rest, gastric decompression, IV ABx, resus)
    • Operative management – if intestinal perforation
      • Stomas – leave proximal and distal end attached to abdo wall
      • Only resect truly necrotic bowel

    • 1 Single lengths of small/large bowel, #2 multiple isolated SI lesions, #3 necrotising enterocolitis totalis – entire bowel

    • Complications  Short gut, long term TPN, intestinal failure and death.
    • 15-30% mortality rate, 50% in those requiring surgery
