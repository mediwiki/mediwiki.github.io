Malrotation/Volvulus (Paediatrics)

General features

  • 1% of population may have intestinal malrotation, diagnosed in 1st year of life (7-90%)

Pathogenesis

  • Midgut: DJ junction to mid-TV colon - supplied by SMA (running in root of mesentery)
  • In gestation
    • Midgut elongates into umbilical sac, returns to intra-abdomina posiiton (10wk GA) → Mesenteric root rotates CCW during retraction (→ colon crosses abdominal cavity ventrally, Cecum moves from left to right lower quadrant, duodenum crosses dorsally, and becomes partly retroperitoneal)
    • Incomplete rotation → rdsal fixation of mesentery is defective and shortened
      • bowel from ligament of Treitz to mid-TV colon rotates around narrow mesenteric root → SMA occlusion (volvulus)
      • Narrow based mesentery: predisposes to volvulus – starts outside the foetus and comes back in at 12 weeks
  • Twists around mesentery and vascular supply → ischemia, infarction, perforation, necrosis → lethargy, abdominal distension, bloody stools

Clinical features

  • Previously healthy 1/12 infant
  • Sensitive: Bilious vomit in 1st week, failure to pass meconium
  • Irritability with abdominal pain
  • Bowel ischemia within 4 hours → rapid peritonitis, sepsis, shock and death.

Diagnosis and investigations

  • Plain AXR - paucity of distal gas (distal to duodenum).
  • Upper GI contrast study: DJ flexure should be to the left of the spine and ascend to the pyloric level → C shaped duodenum
    • If the duodenum is to the right of the spine and “S” shape → Surgery
    • "S" shape - corkscrew sign

Treatment

  1. Broaden the mesenteric base
    • Ladd’s procedure: Untwist the bowel and divide peritoneal adhesions to broaden the base of the mesentery.
      • Typically take appendix – caecum becomes LUQ
  2. Remove dead/necrotic bowel
    • Can leave behind suspect bowel behind and go for a re-look at the suspect bowel (second look) after 24h - aim to retain as much bowel as possible
  3. Manage side effects
    • Short bowel syndrome: <30-40% of remaining bowel
      • will be dependent on TPN → may need small bowel transplantation
