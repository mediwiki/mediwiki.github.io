Hernias

Types of hernias in children

  • Umbilical hernia –  don’t fix until 4 or 5 –  heal on their own
    • Neck is important – >2cm fix (push back in) – esp. if black
    • Contents: omentum – possibly bowel, rarely non-reducible
  • Paraumbilical: swelling arises from above → smile sign – do not resolve spontaneously
    • Contents: epigastric fat, rarely non-reducible – but more likely to cause pain and discomfort
  • Inguinal hernia –  direct = lifestyle, indirect –  through the canal
    • Erythema over the labia – ovarian torsion
    • Contents: bowel, ovary, fallopian tube, bladder
    • As long as not tender – fine to send them home for operation in a week
    • Boys affected side – only operate – bilateral if prematurity (↑risk of inguinal hernias)
    • Age is inversely related to risk of complications from the hernia
  • Femoral hernia goes through femoral canal –  small hernia –  more in old ladies
    • Mid inguinal point = femoral artery
    • Below mid point of inguinal ligament = femoral nerve

Examination

  • Can’t get above a hernia –  deep ring and superficial ring down into the scrotum –  if you try and feel it can’t get above it as it goes to the ring
  • Skin swelling – ovarian cyst? US – fluid
    • Prolapse of ovary into inguinal hernia → ovarian tortion
    • <12 months: 50% risk of metachronous inguinal hernia on contralateral side
    • Untort – hopefully pinking up of structure and not much haemodynamic compromise
