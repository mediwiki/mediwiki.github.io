Breast milk, substitutes and introduction of solids

Breast milk, breast milk substitutes and introduction of solids

Preterm breastfed infants require iron supplements from 4–8 weeks of age.

Breastfed infants - improved neurodevelopment & ↓infection, diabetes, necrotizing enterocolitis and gastro-oesophageal reflux

specific immune factors such as IgA, maternal lymphocytes and macrophages, and other non-specific protective factors such as lactoferrin and lysozyme.

All formulas have CMP – modified through heat to resemble human milk + lactose + fat (veg oil)

At 4-6 months solids can be introduced supplementing milk (main source of nutrition until 12 months) - lost the tongue thrust or extrusion reflex, have head control, and are able to sit without support, allowing them to manipulate solid foods

Born with Fe stores for 1st 4-6 months – rice (Fe) at 6 months

  • solids 6 months – chewing reflex develops
  • Iron-fortified infant cereals are usually introduced first
  • Fruit and vegetables are introduced gradually.
  • haem iron such as meat and poultry 7 months of age.

Custard, yoghurt and cheese can also be introduced.
