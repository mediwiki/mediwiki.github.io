Adolescent health

Developmental tasks of adolescence (affected by)

  • Establish a realistic body image (media M & F)
  • Establish a self identity that is realistic, positive & stable
  • Achieve appropriate independence from parents and other adults (prolongation of dependence on family)
  • Integrate into the peer group (isolation)
  • comfortable with one’s own sexuality and intimate relationships (difference & diversity)
  • Acquire skills for a future vocation (access to education)
  • Develop a morality/value system (rapid social change)

Epi

  • Adolescence (2nd decade): 10-19 yrs
  • Puberty: 10 – 16 years (majority) – 3 years to complete
  • Youth (20% popn): 12 -24 years
  • Young adult/emerging adulthood: 18-25 up to 30 years (AYA)
  • Gains in childhood health and wellbeing are not maintained in adolescence – loss in health investment
  • Psycho-social and health risk behaviour drive morbidity
    • main reasons for adolescents seeing GPs are: Respiratory, Skin

Resilience: ability to face and rise above adversity, and to learn

  • Multiple networks & connections to parents, family, school, community institutions, adults; ‘one adult who cares’
  • Development of academic and social competence and positive school ethos
  • Involvement in outside activities that create multiple friendship networks
  • Internal rather than external locus of control – belief have control over their future
