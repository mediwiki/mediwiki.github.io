Febrile neutropenia - Definitions - Fever - PO temperature > 38.3 OR 38.0 for >1h - neutropenia - ANC < 500/uL OR ANC < 1000 with predicted nadir of <500 in next 48h - Risk for occult infection and mortality increases as ANC falls below 1000 - greatest risk with ANC < 500 - mortality rate for solid tumours less than haem malignancies - solid tumour febrile neutropenia vs. leukemia/lymphoma neutropenia better - growth factors (GCSF) MODESTLY reduces neutropenia and hospitalisation (<1 day difference), with no improvement on mortality - no benefit to be using GCSF in uncomplicated febrile neutropenia - Risk factors - Rapid decline in ANC - prolonged duration of neutropenia (>7-10d) - Leukemia induction therapy - Uncontrolled cancers - Haematologic malignancies - Typical infectious sources - Catheters - Skin - Respiratory tract - Sinuses - GI tract - Note that sources identified in less than 30% of cases; when identified, majority (>80%) are from endogenous flora - gram positive infections (50-60%) - s. epidermidis - streptococcus - enterococcus faecalis/faecium - gram negative rods (more likely to cause death) - enterbacteriaceae (e.coli, klebsiella) - pseudomonas aeruginosa - Approach - History - Physical - FBC, EUC, LFT, UA, Blood/sputum/urine cultures - CXR - consider directed radiology - Chest,abdo,sinus CT if warranted by symptoms - Treatment - Empiric antibiotics - broad spectrum with gram positive and gram negative coverage (especially pseudomonal coverage) - 3rd generation cephalosporine (cefepime or ceftazidime) - depends on local protocols - Alternatives - imipenem, cilastatin, meropenem â†’ increased C.diff occurance rate - true beta lactam allergies - cipro + clinda or aztreonam + vanco - <1% cross-reactivity between 3rd gen cephalosporines and pencillin/1st gen cephalosporin allergy - note empirical vancomycin does not improve morbidity or mortality; encourages development of vancomycin resistant enterococcus - vancomycin used when high suspicion of line/skin infection, severe mucositis (oral flora risk), history of MRSA, severe penicillin allergy (given with quinolone or aztreonam) - If started on IV antibiotics, they can be stopped/changed only when BOTH fever and neutropenia have resolved - i.e. ANC > 500 AND afebrile AND source isolated => complete course of antibiotics - ANC > 500 aND afebrile AND no source isolated => discontinue ALL antibiotics - "Low risk" febrile neutropenia - oral antibiotic treatment is appropriate - use cipro/levo + amox/clav - anticipated duration of neutropenia < 7 days - solid tumour - clinicall stable - no major morbidities - adequate oral intake and social supports - malignancy responding to current treatment (i.e. not refractory disease) - observe for a few hours
