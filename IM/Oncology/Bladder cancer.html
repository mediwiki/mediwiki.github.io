Epidemiology

  • M:F = 3:1
  • median age 73-74, older patient
  • 10% will present metastatic → generally incurable, 4% of all cancer deaths
  • Risk factors
    • Age
    • Tobacco Hx
    • Azo dyes
    • Infections - schistosomiasis results in SCC
    • arsenic, cyclophosphamide cancer
    • Race - whites
    • male
    • FHx
    • personal hx of bladder cancer

Types of bladder cancer

  • urotherlial/TCC (90%)
  • Squamous (6-8%) - schistosomiasis
    • chronic catheterisation can result in nonschistosomiasis causes (spinal cord patients)
  • adenocarcinoma (2%) - rare
    • urachal vs. nonurachal (chronically irritated TCC)
  • small cell (<1%)

Diagnosis

  • gross/microscopic hematuria
  • recurrent UTI without clear bug
    • refer to urology for cystocopy upper tract eval
    • send urine cytology
    • consider CTAP

Staging

  • Non-muscle invasive: Most on surface (Ta) or lamina propia (T1)
    • 60% of all cases, treated with urologist
      • risk of progression depends on grade (t1a vs. ta)
    • Management
      • Ta: cystoscopy and TURBT
      • T1 → BCG immunotherapy (stimulates immune system to eliminate cancer) - increased disease free survival, decreases need for salvage cystectomy; no improvement in overall survival
  • Muscle invasive: management changes if muscle invasive (T2), fat (T3a, t3b) - need to always sample muscle layer
    • NEEDS surgery or irradiation
    • organ confined - high chance of cure with surgery
    • LN involvement - median overall 2 years even with surgery
    • SWOG trial - MVAC neoadjuvant chemo - close 3y benefit in overall survival when added to surgery alone
    • adjuvant chemo - immediate treatment has trend towards survival (EORTC trial has some issues) - IF can get cisplatin
    • if nonsurgical (due to comorbidities) - trimodality bladder preservation
      • small solitary lesions <5cm, able to do complete TURBT, no CIS (flat lesion), no hydronephrosis, T2/T3 disease
      • Approach
        • Maximal TURBT → Radiation (64-65Gy bladder, 44-45Gy pelvic nodes) → Cisplatin therapy (weekly vs. q3wkly) OR 5FU/mitocycin carbo/taxol → repeat salvage TURBT (in 20-30%)
        • 50% have long term control with in tact bladder
    • Metastatic: T4 involves abdo/pelvic wall
      • cisplatin is best drug for urothelial cancer (gemcytabine/cysplatin better than MVAC)
      • survival ~ 14-15mo median
      • small number of metastatic cancers are curable
        • 20% may be very chemosensitive and have chance of cure
      • 50% are unfit for cisplatin - would try carboplatin/gemcitabine/taxane → 30-40% response rate (few complete responses)
      • PD1 blockade is next step
        • tumors can express PDL-1; pembrolizumab, atezolizumab, nivolumab, durvalumab, avelumab block PD-1
        • KEYNOTE-045 trial
          • pembrolizumab vs. regular chemotherapy for advanced previously treated urothelial cancer
          • 10.3mo vs. 7.4mo (i.e. ~ 3mo benefit in survival)
