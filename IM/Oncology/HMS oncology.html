Case 9 - Lung cancer staging - Stage M1(IV) or IIIB are unresectable - N1 - ipsilateral peribronchial or hilar (resectable) - N2 - ipsilateral mediastinal or subcarinal (resectable) - N3 - contralateral mediastinal or hilar, ipsilateral or contralateral scalene, or supraclavicular LN (unresectable) - T3 - tumor of any size NOT involving heart, great vessels, trachea, oesophagus, vertebrae, main stem bronchus or carina - T4 (unresectable) - ANY size, involving heart, trachea, oesophagus, vertebra, carina OR separate nodule in a different lobe - M1 - malignant pleural/pericardial effusion

Case 10 - 65M worsening back/hip pain. Bone scan and CAP CT showing widespread blastic bone mets without clear primary. Which blood test most helpful to identify the primary - PSA Evaluating cancer of unknown primary - CT CAP with contrast - unclear if PET-CT superior - may be better for bone mets - CT CAP better for liver - Focused tumor markers - often non-specifically elevated - e.g. adenocarcinoma and bone mets in male → PCA - man with mediastinal/midline carcinoma → germ cell tumor → hCG, AFP - immunohistochemistry vs. tissue of origin molecular profiling - Histology of unknown primaries - Adenocarcinoma (70%) - lung, hepatobiliary, pancreatic, renal most common - poorly difefrentiated (25)% - lymhpoma, melanoma, sarcoma, germ cell - SCC (5)% - cervical => head and neck primary - inguinal => genital or anorectal primary - neuroendocrine (1%) - Aim is to find a non-metastatic cancer - woman wiht axillary lymph nodes has adenocarcinoma - treat as a node positive breast cancer (even if no MRI findings) → curative intent - neck lymph nodes with SCC - note even stage IV SCC - has good treatment outcomes - treat as locally advanced head and neck - young man with mediastinal/retroperitoneal lymphadenopathy - germ cell tumour → treat with curative intent - woman with peritoneal disease - consider as stage III ovarian cancer → even without ovarian primary - Find treatable metastatic cancers - Breast - Ovarian - Prostate - Germ cell - Lymphoma - Melanoma - Head and neck - Colorectal

Case 11 - 45F premenopausal w/ severe hot flushes during day and frequent night sweats. Hx stage 1 ER- BCa diagnosed 2y ago w/ lumpectomy and radiation. Not on hormonal therapy. What is the best choice for treating her hot flushes? - paroxetine - RCTs show 30-50% decrease in frequency and intensity of hot flushes with - SSRIs (paroxetine, fluoxetine) - SNRIs (venlafaxine) - Gabapentin - Clonidine - no consistent effect of soy protein, black cohosh, etc. → no effect in placebo RCTs

Breast cancer survivorship - menopause symptoms common in breast cancer survivors - iatrogenic from therapy - would hormonal therapy be safe to give in BCa survivors? - increased risk of recurrence when given systemically - results are similar regardless of ER status - therefore do NOT give systemic hormonal therapy for breast cancer survivors - CAN consider limited vaginal hormonal therapy (e.g. ring, suppository, cream) due to reduced systemic absorption
