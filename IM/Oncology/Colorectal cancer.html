Colorectal cancer

Background

  • Risk factors
    • Decrease risk - screening, exercise, Vitamin D, aspirin/NSAIDs, post-menopausal estrogen, calcium
      • most decreased risk is from large observational studies rather than RCTs
    • Increased risk - family history, IBD, diabetes, obesity, red meat, western diet, EtOH, smoking
  • Familial/hereditary risk factors
    • Familial syndromes (~5% of CRCs)
      • familial adenomatous polyposis - FAP gene mutation
        • 100s-1000s of polyps by mid-teens, need full colectomy by 20s
      • hereditary nonpolyposis cancer syndrome - Lynch syndrome - mutated mismatch repair gene
        • associated with ovarian and uterine cancer, biliary cancers
        • may have 10s of polyps, at younger ages
    • Family history/personal history cancers (~10-15%)
      • ~ 2x risk of developing CRC if you have family history
        • 1st degree relative - 5% (baseline) → 10% lifetime risk
          • adenomatous polyp also counts as a 'cancer'
  • 80% present without diagnosis of mets

Screening

  • No RCTs looking at colonscopies for asymptomatic patients
    • multiple FOBT trials to support it
  • screening allows early detection and removal of polyps
  • ** Look up australian screening guidelines **

Staging

Treatment

- Surgery considered at all stages

  • Stage II colon cancer
    • data on adjuvant therapy use is mixed - benefit not as much as with stage III disease
  • Rectum is in a fixed structure; if surgery is performed there, higher rates of local recurrence in rectum (even in sharp dissection)
    • radiation reduces this risk
    • rectal cancer gets neoadjuvant chemoradiation and adjuvant chemotherapy
  • Stage IV disease - stage at time of DIAGNOSIS
    • recurrent disease
    • in general not curable but some can be cured; previously 10 months on 5FU, now 2-3 years
    • palliative care involvement

Surgery - 80% present without detectable metastases - colon cancer - at least 12-14 LNs removed - laparoscopic surgeries noninferior to open surgeries in CRC - rectal cancer - low anterior resection - maintains sphincter; upper rectum; - abdominoperineal resection - low tumours, tumor invades into muscle → permanent colostomy

  • radiation RARELY used for colon therapy due to low risk of local recurrence
