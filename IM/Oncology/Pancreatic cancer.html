Pancreatic cancer

Epidemiology

  • survival after pancreatectomy not significantly improved over non-pancreatectomy patients
  • risk factors
    • Hereditary
      • Hereditary chronic pancreatitis
      • Peutz-Jeghers syndrome
      • Ataxia-telangiectasia
      • BRCA2
    • Chronic pancreatitis - mixed data
    • Diabetes (RR = 2)
    • Smoking
    • Obesity
    • History of partial gastrectomy

Treatments

  • Local disease → surgery + adjuvant therapy
    • not affecting surrounding vessels - SMA, SMV, coeliac
    • localised to pancreas only
    • Adjuvant therapy - FOLFIRINOX achieving 30-40% 5-year survival post pancreatectomy
    • curative intent resection in only 15% of pancreatic cancer patients
      • 15% of resected patients will survive 5 years
  • Borderline resectable → Neoadjuvant chemotherapy, reassess, +/- radiotherapy, reassess, surgery if resectable
    • touching the blood vessels or < 180 deg around
    • R0 resection possible in 64% of patients
      • 1/3 of resections had r0 margins, 13% had pathological complete response
  • Locally advanced → chemotherapy +/- chemoradiation
    • fully wrapped around blood vessel
    • no way to get a curative resection (R0 disease)
  • Metastatic → palliative care + chemotherapy
    • gemcitabine replaced 5FU - improved survival
    • Folfirinox (5FU, LV, irinotecan, oxaliplatin) superior to gemcitabine in response rate, medina PFS, OS
    • Gemcitabine-Paclitaxel superior to gemcitabine alone
