Antifungals

  • Fluconazole
    • candida albicans (vaginitis, thrush), cryptococcus
    • not for krusei, glabrata or auris
    • does not cover aspergillus
    • do not use in neutropenic fever - do not know what fungus is in neutropenic fever
    • almost no adverse effects
  • Icanocandins - caspofungin, mycofungin, anudulafungin
    • covers ALL candida

Onychomycosis / Tenia fungin - eficonazole and tavabarole - only topical agents against onychomycosis - terbinafine is better for nail and hair involvement - better efficacy AND fever adverse effects

Aspergillus - voriconazole - better efficacy and fewer adverse effects - AE - visual disturbance - better than amphoteracin for efficacy and adverse effects - also covers all candida - Icanocandins can be used - itraconazole - largely equal to fluconazole, but less easy to use - rarely best initial therapy for anything - used for MINOR aspergillosus (allergic bronchopulmonary aspergillosus)

Isovuconazole - for aspergillus and mucormycosis - mucormycosis - mould eating through face → out of control diabetic - 'risopus' - eats through face; in sweet and acidic environments

Echinocandins - caspo, mica, anidula - excellent for neutropenic fever patients - does NOT cover cryptococcus - reduced mortality compared to amphotericin - MOA: interupts beta-1,3 glycan bond formation → this is not found in human cells → not much toxicity

Amphotericin - mucormycosis and cryptococcus - can be used against all candida, cyptococcus and aspergillus - note in aspergillus - voriconazole is superior - note in neutropenic fever - caspofungin is superior - note in candida - fluconazole is equal for efficacy - adverse effects - fever, shakes, chills - kidney failure - increased SCr, renal tubular acidosis (distal RTA), hypokalaemia + metabolic acidosis - directly toxic to tubules → hyperchloremic RTA (Cl- up due to HCO3- loss) - if this occurs, should be switching to liposomal amphotericin (protects kidneys)

Fungaemia - initial drug CANNOT be fluconazole → fluconazole does NOT cover all candida - start with an eichanofungin - caspofungin or micafungin - can switch to fluconazole AFTER confirmation of species

MOA of antifungals - azoles inhibit coversion of lanosterol to ergosterol - ergosterol is fatty acid in cell wall of fungus - effectively similar MOA to penicillin - disrupting ergosterol increases permeability and causes cell lysis of fungi
