Staphylococcus aureus

Staphlococcus aureus bacteraemia

Background

  • significant morbidity and mortality, requires ID consult
  • ALWAYS assume gram positive cocci in clusters is A. aureus
  • complicated by metastatic infections - consideration must be given regarding treatment of these complications (E.g. cefazolin has poor CNS penetration)
    • endocarditis
    • septic arthritis
    • lung abscess
    • osteomyelitis
    • staphlylococcal pneumonia
    • cardiac implantable electronic device infections
  • can also result in staphylococcal Toxic Shock Syndrome

Management for adults (Empirical)

  1. Immediately start empirical therapy
    • Flucloxacillin 2g q6hrly PLUS Vancomycin 25-30mg/kg IV loading
    • If immediate nonsevere/delayed hypertensitivity- replace flucloxacillin with cefazolin 2g q8hrly IV
  2. Remove source of infection
  3. TTE
  4. PICC line
  5. Antibiotic duration depends on bacteraemia complexity
    • Complicated bacteraemia - 4 weeks IV (up to 6 weeks if slow response):
      • positive blood culture 48h AFTER starting antibiotics
      • fever 72h AFTER starting antibiotics
      • abnormal valvular morphology or evidence of valvular lesions, regurgitation, endocarditis on TTE/TOE
      • no identifable source of infection (or idenified source NOT addressed)
      • evidence of metastatic infection (E.g. vertebral osteomyelitis, endocarditis)
      • IV protheses (e.g. pacemaker, cardiac valve, AV graft)
    • Uncomplicated - 2 weeks IV therapy
  6. Repeat bloods for 48-72h after starting ABx 2nd daily until first negative culture result
  7. Monitor for duration of treatment (FBC, CRP, EUC, LFT)

Management for adults (Targeted)

  • Penicillin-susceptible
    • Benzylpenicillin 1.8g q4hrly IV
  • MSSA
    • Flucloxacillin 2g q6hrly IV
    • cefazoln 2g q8hrly IV
  • MRSA
    • IV Vancomycin

Management for children

  • Uncomplicated MSSA - 7 days IV after first negatibe blood culture
  • Uncomplicated MRSA - 14 days IV
  • Complicated S. Aureus - requires ID advice
