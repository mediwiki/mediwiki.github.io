Meningitis

Background

Acute meningitis

Aetiologies of meningitis include:

  1. Viral Meningitis
  2. Bacterial Meningitis
  3. Fungal Meningitis

Subacute and chronic meningitis

Background

  • Definitions
    • Subacute = 5-30 days
    • Chronic = >30 days
  • Aetiology
    • Mycobacterium tuberculosis and fungi most common causes
    • Tuberculous meningitis
    • Fungal Meningitis
    • Neurobrucellosis
    • Parasitic meningitis
    • Noninfectious meningitis

Clinical features

  • Headaches
  • Photophobia
  • Nausea and vomiting

Diagnosis

  1. Clinical history
  2. LP - MANDATORY
    1. bacterial cultures needed for antibiotic sensitivity
    2. PCR for common viruses/arboviral serologies may be beneficial
  3. Blood tests
    1. Blood cultures - 5% will have a positive gram stain for bacteria/yeast
