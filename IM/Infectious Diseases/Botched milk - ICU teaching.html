Presentation

  • 61M with worsening diplopia, bulbar symptoms, weakness
  • no significant comorbidies or precipitating illnesses
  • onset 8/1, awoke with diplopia
    • ataxia and dysarthria / coordination and balance issues when walking with dog
    • noticed speech and word-finding dififculities → ED presentation in evening
    • no associated headache, back pain, neurological symptoms
    • no fevers, diarrhoea (?GBS), respiratory symptoms
  • PMHx
    • Hypertension - low dose statin and antihypertensive
    • dyslipidaemia
    • no diabetes history
  • Social History
    • worked in business, born in Australia
    • no recent travel
  • On examination
    • bilateral CN6 palsy, L > R
    • diplopia with primary gaze
    • bilateral dysmetria / past pointing
    • unremarkable bloods
    • CT brain, perfusion and angiogram → no acute intracranial pathology
  • Admitted for stroke workup
    • 300mg IV aspirin stat then 100mg OD
    • NBM until speech pathology review to next day
  • Day 1
    • worsening nausea and vomiting
    • worsening bulbar dysfunction
    • MRI ordered but aborted
      • likely aspiration event during MRI scan (vomited during MRI)
      • increasingly hypoxic and intubated later in the night
    • Descending symmetric paralysis (<48h)
  • Day 2
    • MRI-B no acute pathology
    • DDx possible GBS MFS
    • IVIg 10/1 started for possible GBS
  • Day 4
    • CSF - protein 0.35g/L, nil cells seen
  • Day 8
    • Plasma exchange 16/1
    • Nerve conduction studies - pure motor neuropathy with no sensory features (inconsistent with MFS)
  • Day 15
    • History retaken - drank 1L of putrid smelling almond milk (in date) 12-36h prior to symptom onset
    • had 6 x 1L bottles - one other bottle was similar
    • ?Botulism - would you give anti-toxin? No - toxin already fully bound so antitoxin has no effect
    • send
    • No C.botulinum isolated on milk or stool, and toxin gene A-G not detected
    • neurotoxin detection (blood, feces, food) - biofire assay positive for c.botulinum toxin A
  • Day >100
    • episodes of line sepsis, deconditioned
    • ICU discharge over 100 days

Notes - Bilateral CN6 palsy → increased intracranial pressure? Sinus disease, venous sinus thrombosis? - ?Diabetes - mononeuritis multiplex - ?Diarrhoea - GBS causing illness - Lyme disease - Primary - rash - Secondary - facial nerve involvement - Miller Fisher Syndrome of GBS - usually presents with at least 2 of the following features: ataxia, areflexia, and ophthalmoplegia - Bickerstaff brainstem encephalitis (BBE) and involves altered consciousness, ataxia, ophthalmoparesis, and paradoxical hyperreflexia - https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK507717/ - Bulbar palsy/dysfunction - transverse myelitis - undiagnosed myaesthenia - Botulism
