Antivirals

  • Herpesvirus
    • Most accurate test: HSV PCR
    • Best step - treatment
      • acyclovir, valacyclovir, famciclovir - equal efficacy in HSV and VZV
      • use PO formulation (not topical) → antivirals do not work by going on top (they need systemic)
  • CMV - retinal involvement (CMV retinitis)
    • management - valganciclovir, ganciclovir, foscarnet - equal in efficacy
      • also covers HSV and VZV
      • valganciclovir - best long-term therapy for CMV retinitis
        • L-isomer of gangiclovir → can be given orally
      • adverse effects
        • valganciclovir and ganciclovir - neutropenia and BM suppression (especially WCC line)
        • foscarnet - renal toxicity
    • associated with AIDS (< 150 T-cells)
  • Hepatitis C
    • no vaccination available
    • no longer use IFN; simeprevir and moseprevir, ribavirin no longer used
    • options - all oral, no adverse effects, 95-100% efficacy (evne with cirrhosis, decompensated, HIV)
      • sofosbuvir-velpatasvir
      • sofosbuvir-ledipasvir
      • elbasvir-grazoprevir
      • daclatasvir-sofosbuvir
      • ombitasvir-paritaprevir-dasabuvir
      • voxilaprevir added to velpatasvir/sofosbuvir for rare failures
    • no real adverse effects of existing options
  • Influenza
    • first 48 hours (normally resolves in 3-4 days so only use early)
      • amantidine no longer used
      • can use - oseltamivir, zanamivir (neuraminidase inhibitors)
        • inhibitos both influenza A and B
  • Chronic hepatitis B
    • Lamivudine - previously HIV medication
    • Adefovir - failed HIV medication with too much toxicity
    • Tenofovir - first line HIV medication
    • Entecavir
    • Telbivudine - HIV medication (based on sivovudine)
    • Interferon - last agent (painful, needs injection, higher adverse effects)
    • These drugs are not as good as the hepatitis C drugs
      • works in 50% of patients
    • Always used as monotherapy - do not work better in combinations

Other antivirals

  • ribavirin - rarely in hepatitis C, can try against RSV
  • interferon
    • never used first line in infectious diseases
    • causes arthalgia, myalgias; worsens depression; causes flu-like symptoms; low plt and WCCs; thyroid problems

Hepatitis C medication MOAs

  • RNA polymerase inhibitors → similar to the use of reverse transcriptase inhibitors in HIV
  • OR - protease inhibitors → prevent viral maturation by inhibiting protein synthesis
    • virus is nothing but a protein covered DNA
  • if you stop genetic production or protein coating production → can stop Hepatitis C
