Hypokalemia

Clinical Manifestations

  • Typically no signs/symptoms until K+ < 3.0mEq/L
  • Muscular signs/symptoms
    • Weakness (legs > arms), cramps, ileus, rhabdomyolysis
  • Cardiac conduction
    • EKG changes (ST depression, TWI, Prominent U waves)
    • Arrhythmias (AF, VT, TdP) - at higher risk with QT prolonging drugs, Digoxin toxicity, Hypomagnesemia, Coronary ischemia

Aetiology

  1. Poor intake
    1. Starvation
    2. Anorexia
  2. GIT losses
    1. Vomiting/NG drainage
    2. Diarrhoea
    3. Laxative abuse - form of bullemia
    4. Ureterosigmoidostomy - ureter into sigmoid after bladder removal -> increased Na+ delivered to colon
  3. Urinary losses
    1. Diuresis - loop and thiazide diuretics common
    2. Mineralocorticoid excess
      1. Primary hyperaldosteronism - excess aldosterone (e.g. tumour, CAH)
      2. Secondary hyperaldosteronism - excess renin resulting in increased aldosterone (renovascular disease, tumour)
      3. Pseudohyperaldosteronism - excess non-aldosterone mineralocorticoid agonist (Cushings, Exogenous mineralocorticoids)
    3. Renal Tubular Acidosis 1 and 2 (not 4)
    4. Salt wasting nephropathy
    5. Amphotericin B (antifungal) - affects tubular permeability
    6. Polyuria - due to excess K+ moved through renal tubules
    7. Hypomagnesemia
  4. Internal redistribution
    1. Insulin
    2. Catecholamines
    3. Alkalosis
    4. Marked cell proliferation (e.g. initial treatment of VB12 deficiency)
    5. Hypokalemic periodic paralysis - triggered by exercises and fasting or high carb meal (temporary muscle paralysis)

Diagnostic approach

  1. Take history (e.g. diarrhoea, diuretics)
  2. Check Mg
  3. Assess acid-base status

Management

  • Urgency of repletion dependent on rate of hypokalemia development and high risk comorbidities (E.g. EKG changes)

Forms of K+

  1. Oral KCl - indicate for majority
    • Given of can take orally - 40mEq at a time q2-4h
    • Can have GI upset (give with food)
  2. IV KCl
    • Given if NPO
    • 10mEq/hr via peripheral vein or 20mEq/hr via central line
    • Can cause burning pain proximal to IV site
