Kidney Function Evalulation

Biochemical markers

Serum Creatinine

  • Creatinine is freely filtered by glomerulus and secreted into urine by the proximal tubule
    • tubular secretion contribution as proportion of total creatinine increases as GFR declines
      • when urinary creatinine clearance used to estimate GFR, secreted creatinine will contribute to overestimation of true GFR
    • complete loss of kidney function - SCr can increase by 88.4uM per day
  • significant losses in kidney function at high GFR may be masked by small changes in serum creatinine
  • Creatinine
    • metabolite of creatine, mostly present in skeletal muscle
    • high muscle mass (younger, men, black) - higher SCr compared to less muscular people with same GFR
    • loss of muscle mass (Aging, wasting, malnutrition, amputation) results in lower SCr - here, SCr tends to overestimate GFR
    • Medications can reduce proximal tubule secretion of creatinine
      • cimetidine, trimethoprim, cobicstat, dolutegravir, victegravir - elevates SCr despite stable GFR

Serum Cystatin C

  • Cystatin C produced by all nucleated cells
  • Freely filtered by glomeruli and catabolised by tubules
  • Compared to SCr - less affected by age, sex, muscle mass
  • increased by acute disease (e.g. malignancy, hyperthyroid, inflammation, HIV)
  • Better used to estimate EGFR particularly in drug interactions

Blood Urea Nitrogen

  • Product of protein metabolism
  • Levels increase
    • reduced GFR
    • increased urea resorption (occurs in renal hypoperfusion)
    • protein intake and catabolic rate
    • glucocorticoids, haemorrhage, trauma
  • Levels decrease in
    • starvation
    • liver disease

EGFR

  • Variables to take into account
    • Age, race, sex, muscle mass (estimated by weight) and serum creatinine
  • CKD-EPI most accurate for
    • older people
    • EGFR > 60mL/min/1.73m^2
  • CKD-EPI C more accurate but relies on cystatin C value
  • MDRD
    • less accurate at high GFRs
    • performs similar to CKD-EPI at GFR < 60mL/min/1.73m^2
  • Cockcroft-Gault (CGE)
    • least accurate
    • used in drug dosing as it was used in most pharmacokinetic studies
  • To improve creatinine clearance calculations
    • use 24h urine collection
    • radionuclide imaging - most acurate measurement, gold standard in research
