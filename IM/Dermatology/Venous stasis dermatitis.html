Venous stasis dermatitis

Background

  • associated with chronic venous dermatitis
  • weeping plaques with vesicles and crusting
  • can be secondarily infected with bacteria or candida

Clinical features

  • erythematous
  • scaling and eczematous patches or plaques

Management

  • as per Chronic Venous Insufficiency
  • Sparing use of a topical steroid
  • Wound care
    • hydrocolloids and foam dressings - control ulcer trainage and prevent skin maceration

Venous stasis ulcers

Background

  • Account for 70# of all leg ulcers

Pathology

Clinical features

  • History
  • Examination
    • Ulcers mid-calf to ankle, most commonly near medial malleolus
    • associated with signs of Chronic Venous Insufficiency
    • singular or multiple
      • irregularly shaped
      • shaped
      • serous weeping
    • presence of
      • fibrinous tissue
      • rarely necrotic
    • pain may or may not be present
    • concomitant
      • oedema
      • varicosities
      • eczametous changes
      • brown pigementation (Hemosiderin deposition)
      • lipodermatosclerosis (subcutaneous tissue fibrosis)
      • Atrophie Blanche (pale plaques of scar tissue)
      • evidence of healed ulcers

Diagnostic criteria

Investigations

  • Ankle-brachial index
    • rule out concurrent arterial disease

Management

  • Compression therapy (>30mmHg needed)
    • improves venous flow
    • reduces oedema
    • promotes fibrinolysis
    • administered using high-compression multicomponent bandaging, elastic/inelastic compression bandanges (Unna boots, stockings) or intermittent pneumonic compression devices
    • Contraindicated in
      • ABI <= 0.5 (ABSOLUTE contraindication)
  • Local wound care
    • debridement of devitalised tissue
    • Simple non-adherent dressings - as effective as expensive dressings
      • consider adding topical iodine
  • Systemic management
    • Pentoxifylline
    • Simvastatin
    • Aspirin
  • do NOT use antibiotics unless infective signs - e.g. erythema, tenderness at ulcer, lymphangitis, rapidly increasing ulcer size and fever
