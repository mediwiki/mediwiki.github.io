Stroke secondary prevention

Vascular protection

  1. Hypertension
    1. single most modifiable risk factor for ischemic AND haemorrhagic stroke
    2. Aim 120-130/80-90mmHg
      1. Consider adding small dose ACEi if BP already low
      2. avoid using betta blockers as first line unless IHD present
    3. Initiate antihypertensive therapy 24-48h after stroke
  2. Dyslipidaemia
    1. for each 1mmol/L reduction in LDL, 21% relative risk reduction in stroke
    2. SPARCL study - atorvastatin 80mg > placebo (but increased risk of ICH)
    3. Aim for targets
      1. Aim LDL target < 1.8mmol/L
  3. Insulin resistance
  4. Antiplatelet therapy
    1. Aspirin, clopidogrel, or asasantin
    2. CHANCE/POINT - DAPT (Aspirin + clopidogrel) for 3 weeks > aspirin alone
    3. start as soon as CTB excludes haemorrhage (or 24h after tPA + CTB)
    4. Make sure you go back to SAPT after 3 weeks - no long term benefit

Large vessel occlusions

  • Indications for carotid endarterectomy
    • 70-99% stenosed and nondisabling carotid artery stroke/TIA
    • 50-69% if carotid stenosis symptomatic
  • CEA to be done within 2 weeks, at latest < 3mo
  • Indications for stenting
    • Age < 70y/o
    • unfavourable anatomy, asymptomatic restenosis post endarterectomy or previous radiotherapy

Intracranial atherosclerosis

  • Highest rates amongst asian patients
  • symptomatic 50-99% stenosis of intracranial vessels at ~13% risk of recurrent stroke in 2y regardless of antiplatelet use
    • use aspirin - WASID trial - no benefit for warfarin
    • no role of intracranial stenting due to complications (SAMMPRIS trial)
  • Treat with aggressive medical management
