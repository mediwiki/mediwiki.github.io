Lacunar infarct

Background

  • Small lesions (typically < 1.5cm diameter)
  • Occur in distrubtion of short penetrating arterioles in basal ganglia, pons, cerebellum, internal capsule, thalamus and sometimes deep cerebral white matter
  • Associations
    • Poorly controlled hypertension
    • Diabetes

Clinical features

  • Progression may occur over 24-36h prior to stabilising

  • Contralateral Pure motor hemiparesis

    1. 33-50% of lacunar strokes
    2. affects the Basis Pontis (posterior limb of internal capsule, or anterior pons)
  • Ipsilateral Ataxic hemiparesis
    1. Affected posterior limb of internal capsule, basis pontis and corona radiata
    2. combined cerebellar/motor syndrome
    3. Usually affects arms > legs - also known as homolateral ataxia and crural paresis
  • Dysarthria with clumsy hands
    1. affects basis pontis
  • Pure hemisensory defect
    1. Thalamic infarct
  • Mixed sensorimotor
    1. Thalamic and posterior internal capsule stroke

Management

  • As per TIA and cerebral infarct
  • Goal is mainly
    • rehabilitation
    • secondary prevention

Prognosis

  • Good - partial/complete resolution over 4-6 weeks
