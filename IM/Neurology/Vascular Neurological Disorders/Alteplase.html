Alteplase

Pharmacodynamics

  • Tissue plasminogen activator (tPA)

Indication

  • Patients presenting with no evidence of haemorrhagic or large infarct on noncon CT brain
  • Must be applied within 4.5h from onset of stroke
    • Recovery of stroke OR 1.15-1.75
    • within 3 hours - 23.1 vs 32.9% improvement for placebo vs tPA respectively

Dosage

  • 0.9mg/kg alteplase
    • 10% as bolus, 90% over 1 hour
  • ENCHANTED study (NEJM 2016)
    • Q: use of 0.6mg/kg vs 0.9mg/kg alteplase
    • shown to be NOT non-inferior at lower dose, howwever less tPA associated ICH

Risks

  • Intracranial haemorrhage risk increases (3-6%)
    • increased mortality secondary to symptomatic ICH (2.2-2.7%)
    • Risk factors for haemorrhagic transformation:
      • Large infarctions
      • established infarcts
      • Grey matter infarcts
      • Higher NIHHS score
      • poor collateral circulation
      • Hyperglycaemia
      • Thrombocytopenia

Benefits

  • reduces door to needle time - reduces 1 year all cause mortality and re-admission

Contra-indications - absolute

  1. Extensive hypoattenuation on CT
  2. Suspicion of SAH
  3. current/previous ICH
  4. intracranial neoplasms
  5. Severe head trauma in last 3 months
  6. Intracranial or intraspinal surgery
  7. Platelet < 100
  8. INR > 1.7
  9. APTT > 40
  10. Clexane last 24h
  11. Suspicion for current endocarditis
  12. Active GI or internal bleed
  13. Aortic arch dissection

Contra-indications - relative

  1. Mild and non-disabling symptoms
  2. Stroke in last 3/12
  3. Large or giant aneurysm, other intracranial vascular malformations
  4. Bleeding diatheses - renal failure, liver failure, haematological issues
  5. NOACs (need to check factor Xa assays)
  6. Major surgery/trauma last 14 days
  7. Anterior STEMI last 3 months
  8. Arterial puncture of noncompressible site
  9. Post-partum period
