Abdominal imaging

  • Renal tract ultrasound

Case 1

  • Presentation

    • 65M with 5 month history of left flank discomfort, abdominal fullness
    • 2/52 hx LL oedema, 4/7 Hx macroscopic hematuria with clots
    • O/E
      • LLQ abdominal mass, pitting oedema mid-shins bilaterally

  • DDx

    • Kidney mass? RCC? PKD?
  • Ix - https://www.urology-textbook.com/renal-cell-carcinoma-diagnosis.html
    • Labs: FBC EUC CMP LFT, LDH, AP, Coags
    • Ix: Renal/liver ultrasound
      • DDx cystic vs. solid mass

Findings:

  • Solid mass (outlined with dots)

Further investigations

  • Suspicious for RCC/mass - for abdominal CT w/ multiphase contrast
    • Purpose:
      • Determine management (Sx or chemotherapy)
      • Help determine prognosis

The findings on imaging

  • Portal venous phase study
  • Left kidney inferior pole partly exophytic renal mass with peripheral enhancement and central necrosis.
  • No invasion of renal hilum

Case 2

  • 45M 1hr hx sudden left sided flank pain radiating towards groin w/ N+V
    • BG CKD (baseline eGFR 40)
  • DDx
    • Renal colic - ?nephrolithasis
    • ?PKD ruptured cyst
    • ? flank tenderness/fever ?infection (pyelonephritis)
  • Ix
    • eGFR 23, otherwise all other values within normal ranges
      • Interpretation → acute on chronic AKI
    • Imaging
      • CT KUB for suspected nephrolithasis - non-contrast study
      • Alternative: Renal tract US for pregnant/pediatric

  • Abnormality
    • Red: Hydronephrosis
    • Yellow: Hydroureter
    • Green: Renal calculus
      • Common obstructions at the PUJ, crossing of iliac vessels, VUJ

Purpose of imaging in this case:

  • Determine size and consequently if able to manage conservatively
  • Look for complications: obstruction/hydronephrosis, perinephric stranding id suspected infection
  • Determine type of calculus – urate versus oxalate (density, uncomplicated urate stones may be managed more conservatively)
  • What the most appropriate surgical approach would be

Case 3

  • 56M increased abdominal girth, denies jaundice
    • BG IVDU
    • PE: Palpable firm liver (Hepatomegaly), shifting
