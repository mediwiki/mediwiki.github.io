Malignant ascites case

Important topics to review

  • Ascites
  • Malignancies associated with ascites
  • Familial cancer syndromes

Case presentation

Background

Robyn is a 42 year old mother of three teenage children who presents to her GP in Kempsey complaining of increased abdominal distension and weight loss, together with increasing exertional dyspnoea

Presenting complaint

  • Increased abdominal distension, weight loss
  • increased exertional dyspnoea
  • She has not been to the GP in the past few years for acute illness but has had pap smears regularly (no abnormality detected)
  • Associated
    • regular periods
    • on OCP
  • BP normal on previous visits
  • Systems review NAD - post-meal bloating and mild fatigue
  • nil changes in bowel habits or pain

Questions

  1. What are some causes for unintentional weight loss?
    • GI causes (ulcer, coeliac, colitis)
    • Cancer
    • Endocrine (thyroid, DM, adrenal)
    • Psychological/psychiatric (e.g. depression)
    • Other systemic diseases (heart failure, chronic liver diseases, renal disease, autoimmune diseases, chronic infections)
    • malnutrition
    • appetite suppression

Medications history

  • On OCP
  • otherwise nil regular meds

FHx

  • Nil Hx bowel cancer, not on bowel cancer screening
  • breast cancer in mother and elder sister - both alive

Questions

  1. Is the family history of cancer relevant?
    • https://www.bci.org.au/breast-cancer-information/fact-sheets/family-history-breast-cancer/

Social history

  • Stay at home mum
  • moderate physical activity - tennis, walking
  • Mother to teenagers
  • Ex-smoker (20PYHx), nil alcohol consumption

Examination

  • Nil jaundice, rahes
  • Alert, oriented x3
  • Vitals NAD, afebrile
  • Weight 54kg (previously 62kg 12 months ago)
  • Moderate distension of abdomen, non-tender
  • shifting dullness, nil organomegaly or lymphadenopathy
  • dullness and decreased AE on R) base extending to 1/3rd of chest posteriorly - otherwise chest clear to auscultation
  • nil peripheral oedema or leg swelling/tenderness or abnormal signs on CVD exam

Questions

  • Other examinations?
    • Ascites + significant weight loss â†’ examine for common malignances
    • Breast exam NAD
    • PR and PV NAD
    • Urinalysis - to exclude hematuria

Investigations

  • UA normal

Questions

  • Should we do tumour markers?
    • Not diagnostic on their own - can SUPPORT a highly suspicious diagnosis, or for tracking
  • Iron studies?
    • Useful to assess anemia
  • Issues with less targeted approach for investigation?
    • Overdiagnosis

Further management/investigations

  • CT AP
  • Pleural tap

Further questions/discussion

  • What are the potential mechanisms of malignant ascites?
  • What are the commonest malignancies associated with this?

Ongoing management

  • Robyn comes back to see you to discuss her investigation results. She is already aware you are pretty concerned after you let her know that there were some abnormal findings on her chest xray and organised the CT.
  • How are you going to convey the investigation findings to Robyn?
  • You will need to discuss the need for an oncology referral to discuss potential treatment options
  • What important information will you convey in your referral letter to the oncologist?
