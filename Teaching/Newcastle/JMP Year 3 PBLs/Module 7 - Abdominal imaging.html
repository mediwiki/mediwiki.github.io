Abdominal imaging

  • Renal tract ultrasound

Case 1

  • Presentation

    • 65M with 5 month history of left flank discomfort, abdominal fullness
    • 2/52 hx LL oedema, 4/7 Hx macroscopic hematuria with clots
    • O/E
      • LLQ abdominal mass, pitting oedema mid-shins bilaterally

  • DDx

    • Kidney mass? RCC? PKD?
  • Ix - https://www.urology-textbook.com/renal-cell-carcinoma-diagnosis.html
    • Labs: FBC EUC CMP LFT, LDH, AP, Coags
    • Ix: Renal/liver ultrasound
      • DDx cystic vs. solid mass

Findings:

  • Solid mass (outlined with dots)

Further investigations

  • Suspicious for RCC/mass - for abdominal CT w/ multiphase contrast
    • Purpose:
      • Determine management (Sx or chemotherapy)
      • Help determine prognosis

The findings on imaging

  • Portal venous phase study
  • Left kidney inferior pole partly exophytic renal mass with peripheral enhancement and central necrosis.
  • No invasion of renal hilum

Case 2

  • 45M 1hr hx sudden left sided flank pain radiating towards groin w/ N+V
    • BG CKD (baseline eGFR 40)
  • DDx
    • Renal colic - ?nephrolithasis
    • ?PKD ruptured cyst
    • ? flank tenderness/fever ?infection (pyelonephritis)
  • Ix
    • eGFR 23, otherwise all other values within normal ranges
      • Interpretation → acute on chronic AKI
    • Imaging
      • CT KUB for suspected nephrolithasis - non-contrast study
      • Alternative: Renal tract US for pregnant/pediatric

  • Abnormality
    • Red: Hydronephrosis
    • Yellow: Hydroureter
    • Green: Renal calculus
      • Common obstructions at the PUJ, crossing of iliac vessels, VUJ

Purpose of imaging in this case:

  • Determine size and consequently if able to manage conservatively
  • Look for complications: obstruction/hydronephrosis, perinephric stranding id suspected infection
  • Determine type of calculus – urate versus oxalate (density, uncomplicated urate stones may be managed more conservatively)
  • What the most appropriate surgical approach would be

Case 3

  • 56M increased abdominal girth, denies jaundice
    • BG IVDU
    • PE: Palpable firm liver (Hepatomegaly), shifting dullness (ascites)
  • Ix
    • Labs: LFT - transaminitis, hepatocellular injury

Imaging request

  • Abdominal ultrasound (liver ultrasound) +/- aspiration

Alternative imaging modalities

  • CTAP, multiphase CT

The findings on imaging

  • Red circle: Hypoechoic area, consistent with fluid, likely ascites
  • Yellow circle and green circle: surface nodularity
  • Splenomegaly (indicating portal hypertension) can also be seen on USS as can the portal vein (might see thrombosis and can use doppler imaging to determine direction of portal venous flow which is reversed in severe portal hypertension)
  • Findings here are consistent with cirrhosis and ascites

Further imaging

  • CTAP - if suspicious mass found
    • unexplained weight loss, fever, ascites without portal hypertension, or other red flags present

- Red circle: area of low attenuation, consistent with fluid - Green circle: splenomegaly - Blue circle – right lobe shrinkage - Purple circle – caudate lobe hypertrophy - A ascites, D diaphragm, P pleural space

Case 4

  • 45F gradual onset N+V, constipation and abdominal distension
    • PSHx appendectomy 2y prior
    • PE - nil peritonism, tinkling bowel sounds

Imaging to request

  • AXR - assess bowel obstruction, fecal loading
  • Follow with CTAP if obstructed

- The findings on imaging - Confirms diagnosis of small bowel obstruction, cut off in right iliac fossa (likely adhesions) - Other features of obstruction - Lack of gas in the rectum - Discuss features of small bowel and large bowel obstruction - Normal small bowel <3cm - Normal large bowel <6cm

  • How imaging would change management
    • Confirm diagnosis and severity of obstruction
    • Help determine cause
    • Operative or non-operative management (adhesive SBO may be managed conservatively in some), help plan operative therapy

Progress

  • Admitted to ward, conservativelyt managed
  • 16/24 - significant abdominal pain, tachypnoea, tachycardic

  • PE - generalised peritonism

  • DDx

    • perforated viscus w/ peritonism

  • Ix

    • CXR erect
    • can be lateral decubitus if unwell

  • pneumoperitoneium - surgical emergency, for emergency laparotomy
