Case

65F with acute sharp pleuritic chest pain, fairly health with obesity, hypertension, hypercholesterolaemia. Dyspnoeic, tachycardiac, SpO2 90%, nil prior pulmonary history

-- Lateral scan

--

- could trace out left hemidiaphragm 1 year ago

- could see increased lucency as you trace spine 1 year ago (the spine sign) - indication of consolidated lung

  • imp: retrocardiac pneumonia, confirmed on CT chest

Case

45F 2 week hx of cough and low grade fever. Hypoxic and tachypnoeic. Lung sounds crackly

-- - increased interstitial markings, nil obvious pneumonia - diffuse interstitial markings

  • imp: atypical pnuemonia
  • diagnosis: AIDS with PCP

Case

-- - patchy white hilar region on R)

-- - Diagnosis - PCP pnuemonia - DDx - infective endocarditis - metastasis

Case

72F with severe onset SOB.

-- - cardiomegaly - increased interstitial markings - cephalisation

  • Imp: Pulmonary oedema

-- - vessels should be smaller at the top due to gravity - if upper lobe vessels appear similar in diameter to lower lobe, this is a reflection of increased back pressure (reduced venous return) - thickened fissure can be seen - indication of oedema - perihilar fullness/fluffiness - hazy - everything becomes more indistinct

--

- Kerley B lines - engorged interlobular septa commonly seen with pulmonary oedema

--

  • Incomplete aortic ring at aortic arch
    • calcification but not continuous
    • most specific CXR finding for aortic dissection

-- 70F had an aortic dissection, developed an aortic flap which reached aortic root, resulting in her pulmonary oedema
