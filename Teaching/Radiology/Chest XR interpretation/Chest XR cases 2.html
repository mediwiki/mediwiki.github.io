LIFTL Case 1

A 22 yo male presents with worsening chronic cough and breathlessness. He has been underweight since childhood.

  • Details
    • Name - NA
    • AP/PA/Lateral - NA
    • Date of study - NA
  • Basics
    • Rotation - good rotation
    • Inspiration - good inspiration, 10th posterior rib at MCL
    • Picture - good
    • Exposure - good exposure, spinous processes visible
  • A-F approach
    • Airways - trachea midline, deviated R) near aortic knuckle. R) deviation of carina and main bronchi.
      • upper bronchial wall thickening with ring shadows and patches of consolidation
    • Bones - no obvious fractures noted. increased adiposity. acromioclavicular joints and rotator cuffs not appropriately visible for comment.
    • Cardiomediastinum - cardiac borders fuzzy on either side. patchy opacification of hilum in both lung fields.
    • Diaphragm - scalloping appearance of diaphragm. flattened. R higher than L diaphragm as expected. costophrenic angles clear, cardiophrenic angles fuzzy.
      • flattened, features if mild airflow obstruction
    • Extras - nil attachments.
    • Fields - hazy opacification across both lung fields.

-- - hazy opacification posterior to heart and increased radiolucency in posterior lung fields - ring shadows and clear upper lobe predominance of bronchiectasis

Finding

  • Cystic bronchiectasis

LITFL Case 2

44 yo man has routine CXR after possible faint

  • Details
    • Name - NA
    • AP/PA/Lateral - NA
    • Date of study - NA
  • Basics
    • Rotation - rotation appropriate, perhaps mildly rotated to L
    • Inspiration - appropriate, anterior rib 6 at MCL
    • Picture - Good
    • Exposure - appropriate, can see spinous processes at T4
  • A-F approach
    • Airways - trachea midline with mild L) sided angulation. carina and main bronchi midline and acceptable
    • Bones - nil obvious fractures or soft tissue injury. large body habitus.
    • Cardiomediastinum - clear cardiac burder with heart on R) (?dextrocardia). cardiac size normal. Aortic knuckle R) sided.
    • Diaphragm - acceptable. cardiophrenic angles and costophrenic angles visible. Gastric bubble right sided
    • Extras - NAD
    • Fields - fissures vieible but not pronounced. nil obvious cephalisation or vessels or kerley B lines. hilar opacifties similarly positioned and equal density bilaterally.
      • coarse thickened bronchial markings and small ring shadows notably in upper lobes consistent with bronchiectasis

Findings

  • Dextrocardia
    • right sided stomach bubble may suggest situs inversus
  • Kartagener syndrome - primary ciliary dyskinesia
