  • ST: NA, borderline elevated V2
  • T: TWI avR, V1
  • U: flat, V4-V5
  • Other: Baseline wander III, aVL, aVF
  • Interpretation: Normal ECG

Comments: low amplitude normal U waves (< 25% amplitude of preceding T wave, maximum 1.5mm); U wavers easier to see in RHS precordial leads. U waves probably from repolarisation of His-purkinje system or papillary muscles - inverted U-waves from IHD or left ventricular volume overload - prominent U-waves from hypokalemia, hypercalcaemia, digitalis, class 1A and 3 antiarrhythmics, thyrotoxicosis, ICH, exercise, congential long QT
