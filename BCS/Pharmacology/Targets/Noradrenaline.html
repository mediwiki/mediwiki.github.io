Noradrenaline

Uses

  • Typical uses
  • Off-label uses

Pharmacodynamics

  • Sympathomimetic - agonist on alpha1, alpha2, beta1
    • little/no effect on beta2 or dopamine receptors
  • Effect
    • positive inotrope - alpha effect - peripheral vasoconstriction
    • positive chronotrope

Pharmacokinetics

  • A
    • Rapid onset 1-2 minutes
  • D
  • M - COMT and MAO
  • E - fast elimination - half life 3 minutes

Risks, Benefits and considerations

  • Benefits
  • Risks
  • Black box warning??
  • Considerations

Indications

  • Acute hypotension requiring. vasoconstrictor

Contraindications

  • Relative contraindications
  • Absolute contraindications
    • Hypotension from hypovolemia - correct prior to initiating otherwise severe visceral vasoconstriction can result in hypoxia
    • hypertension

Precautions

  • Extravasation - necrosis and sloughing - treat with phentolamine (5-10 mg diluted in 10-15 mL NaCl)
  • profound hypoxia or hypercapnia - can sensitise myocardium to noradrenaline and increase VT/VF risk
  • Mesenteric/peripheral vascular thrombus - can extend infarction

Interactions

  • TCAs - dysrhythmias, hypertension
  • ergot derivatives - vasoconstriction and severe hypertension
  • MAO inhibitors - phenelzine, tranylcypromine, moclobemide, selegiline, linezolid
  • hypertensive/vasoconstriction and arrhythmogenic drugs
  • alpha 1 blockers - prazosin, phenoxybenzamine, phentolamine - can lead to hypotension

IV Administration

  • 6mg/100mL Noradrenaline in 100mL glucose 5% (60mcg/mL)
    • Start at 1-3mcg/minute and increase every 3-5 minutes
    • titrate to adequate blood pressure
    • Max rate 10mcg/min CCU or 20mcg/min in ICU/critical care areas
  • Monitor LFT, EUCs and physical exams/telemetry for hypertension or peripheral ischemia

Adverse effects

  • Common - anxiety, palpitations, headache
  • Infrequent - hypertension, ischemia, bradycardia (reflex), arrhythmias, extravasation
  • Rare - allergy (to sodium metabisulfite)
