Amiodarone

Chemical structure

  • 2 atoms of iodine
  • structural analogue to thyroxine

Pharmacodynamics

  • Class I, II, III, IV actions
  • I - some degree of Sodium blockade
  • II - noncompetitive antiadrenergic drug
  • III - mainly Class III antiarrhythmic
  • IV - calcium channel blocking activity

Pharmacokinetics

  • 22-86% oral bioavailability
    • increased with food
    • may take weeks to months before interactions fully develop due to long half life
  • Distribution - ?
  • Metabolism - Liver
    • Amiodarone and metabolites INHIBIT CYP2C9, CYP2D6, CYP3A4, P-glycoprotein
    • Metabolised by CYP3A4, CYP2C8, P-gp
  • Elimination half-life ~ 40 days - 100 days (depending on tissue distribution)

Indications

  • Suppression of VT (2nd/3rd line)
  • Maintain sinus rhythm in paroxysmal AF/AFlutter
  • Rate control of AF/AFlutter (when other drugs are contraindicated)
  • Cardioversion of AF/Aflutter into sinus rhythm
  • Treatment of AVNRT or bypass-track mediated SVTs

Contraindications

  • 2nd or 3rd degree heart block WITHOUT pacemaker
  • Symptomatic bradycardia WITHOUT pacemaker
  • Sick sinus syndrome WITHOUT pacemaker
  • Allergy to amiodarone or iodine

Other considerations

  • Electrolyte disturbances should be corrected prior to initiating amiodarone
  • Amiodarone has many drug interactions
  • Amiodarone can prolong QT interval and risk TdP -
    • ensure QTc < 500ms
    • avoid if prolonged QT risk factors are present - genetic abnormalities, electrolyte disturbances, increased age, female gender, bradycardia, feart failure, CAD, some drugs
  • Amiodarone can result in atropine resistance bradycardia if used excessively
  • Thyroid dysfunction (including goitre or nodules ) -has increased risk of hypo/hyperthyroidism
  • Lung disease (particularly reduced diffusion capacity) - risk of pulmoanry fibrosis which can worsen existing function
  • Hepatic failure - needed for amiodarone clearance
  • Elderly - can result in symptomatic bradycardia
  • Severe hypotension - can be worsened with amiodarone, likely related to solvents (Polysorbate-80 and benzyl alcohol)
  • Medications to avoid with amiodarone
    • Increase concentrations due to CYP inhibition by amiodarone - docetaxel, doxorubicin, flecainide, paclitaxel, pazopanib
    • increases amiodarone concentrations - grapefruit juice, sodium fusidate
      • HIV drugs: Atazanavir, Darunavir, Fosamprenavir, Indinavir, Lopinavur, Ritonavir, Saquinavir, Tipranivir
      • Sofosbuvir; needs 48h cardiac monitoring then daily monitoring for 2 weeks
    • Antagonises effect - Agalsidase alfa/beta
  • Medications that need dosage reduction
    • Ciclosporin, dabigatran, digoxin, phenytoin, tacrolimus,warfarin
    • Eplerenone, simvastatin

IV Amiodarone administration

  • Central line preferred over peripheral line
    • peripheral line can result in thrombophlebitis - if necessary, use 18 gage, proximal to antecubital fossa, for < 24 hours, with hourly checking
  • dilute with glucose 5% only (incompatible with NaCl)
  • Amiodarone is stable for 24h inside PVC bags - no need to use glass container infusers
  • IV Loading
    • 5mg/kg (up to 300mg) diluted in 5% glucose administered by IV over 20 minutes
    • max total dose is 20mg/kg (up to 1200mg) over 24h
  • IV maintenance infusion over 24h
    • Amiodarone 15mg/kg (up to 900mg) in 500mL glucose 5% over 24h
  • IV breakthrough (bolus) - given in ICU when breakthrough arryhthmias occur
    • 2mg/kg (up to 120mg) bolus over 20 minutes

  • Amiodarone protocols

    • Note that the maximum dose in 24h is 1200mg (including loading, maintenance, breakthrough, and PO/enteral dosing considering bioavailability)
    • Expected loads (can be across multiple days)
      • AF/SVT - up to 5-7g total load
      • VT - 8-10g
    • Taper dosages down to a maintenance of 100-200mg OD
    • After the first 24h bag of amiodarone:
      • Early reversion of arrhythmia
        • no further dosing
        • switch to PO amiodarone loading with view to switch to maintenance
        • cannot switch to PO dosing, so continue low dose IV amiodarone (300mg amiodarone in 500mL glucose over 24h)
      • No/poor response → Further 24h bag required
    • Switching to PO amiodarone
      • Start at 200mg TDS PO (400mg TDS for VT) and with food
      • Need to aim for 100-200mg PO OD by discharge

  • Side effects

    • Common - N+V, hypotension (IV), constipation, anorexia, taste disturbance
      • Visual changes, photosensitivity
      • Abnormal LFTs
      • Hypo/hyperthyroidism → amiodarine chemically similar to thyroxine
      • thrombophlebitis at injection site
      • Bradycardia
      • Neurological symptoms - peripheral neuropathy and myopathy
      • Pulmonary fibrosis - baseline CXR and TFTs are needed prior to long term commencement
      • Sleep disturbance - vivid dreams, nightmares
    • Infrequent
      • AV block
      • Arrhythmias
      • Phlebitis
      • Epididymitis
    • Rare
      • Hepatotoxicity (fibrosis, hepatitis) - can be fatal
      • Optic neuropathy, Reversibly corneal microdeposits, neuritis
      • Bronchospasm
      • Alveolar haemorrhage
      • Heart failure
      • ARDS
      • TdP, severe bradycardia
      • Thrombocytopenia
      • Allopecia, rash
      • SIADH

  • Additional notes

    • Slow onset of action (with PO form)
    • Large volume of distribution
    • Extremely long half-life (2-8 weeks) once steady state achieved
      • Loaded over weeks at higher dose because of this
    • Should be considered a 2nd/3rd line medication when used long term due to side effect profile
    • Despite QT prolongation, rarely causes arrhythmia (TdP) due to the prolongation
    • The use of amiodarone is limited by a number of factors
      • very long half-life (20-100 days). For this reason, loading doses are frequently used
      • should ideally be given into central veins (causes thrombophlebitis)
      • has proarrhythmic effects due to lengthening of the QT interval
      • interacts with drugs commonly used concurrently (p450 inhibitor) e.g. Decreases metabolism of warfarin
      • numerous long-term adverse effects (see below)
    • Monitoring of patients taking amiodarone
      • TFT, LFT, U&E, CXR prior to treatment
      • TFT, LFT every 6 months
