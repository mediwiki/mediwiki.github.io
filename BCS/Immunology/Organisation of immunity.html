Organisation

The immune system is organised on two levels 1. Systemic level - bloodstream, thymus, bone, spleen 1. defends INTERNALLY 2. thymus, bone marrow are central organs of immunity 1. all cells related to immunity originate from bone marrow 2. t-cells mature in thymus, b-cells mature in bone marrow 3. thymus governs the whole immune system 3. spleen, lymph nodes, lymphoid elements, appendix, lymphatics, skin, liver are secondary/peripheral organs of immunity 4. mucosae-associated lymphoid tissue (MALT) organises some essential secondary organs of immunity 2. Skin and mucosal level 1. surface and mucosal barriers, tonsils, adenoids, peyer's patches, solitary/isolated follicles, appendix, lymph nodes, lymph vessels

Classification of immune system cells by function

  1. Antigen-presenting cells (APCs)
    1. dendritic cells
    2. macrophages
    3. B-cells
  2. Immunoregulatory cells
    1. Natural T-regulatory cells (nTreg) and induced subsets
    2. natural B-reculatory cells (nBreg)
    3. Adaptive helper T-cells (TH1, TH2, folliculat Th, folliculat Treh, Th9, Th17, Th22)
  3. Effector cells
    1. inflammatory CD4+ T-cells
    2. cytotoxic CD8+ T-cells
    3. plasma cells
    4. γōT cells
    5. NKT cells
    6. innate lymphoid cells (ILCs) incl. NK cells, monocytes, macrophages, neutrophils, eosinophils, mast cells
  4. Memory cells
    1. Memory CD4+ T-cells
    2. Memory CD8+ T cells
    3. Memory b-cells
    4. plasma cells
