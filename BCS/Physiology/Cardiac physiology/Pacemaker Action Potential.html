Pacemaker Action Potential

Pacemaker activity occurs in the SA and AV nodes. There are 3 phases to its activity:

  1. Phase 0: Upstroke of membrane potential due to opening of Ca2+ channels. As the resting potential of pacemakers is lower than of normal cells, the Na+ channel is permanently inactivated.
    • conduction velocity is slow (not a rapid AP) → results in the slowing of transmission at the AV node, between atrium and ventricles
  2. There is no Phase 1 or Phase 2
  3. Phase 3: Repolarisation by inactivation of Ca2+ channels (to stop continuous depolarisation); and activation of K+ channels, resulting in efflux of + charge (re-establishment of a negative charge)
  4. Slow spontaneous depolarisation occurs due to funny currents at the funny channel
    • these channels are a slow mixed Na+ and K+ influx - this is the primary channel responsible for the automaticity and the pacemaker activity of these cells.
    • the slope of phase 4 determines HR in the SA node.
    • Slope increased with catecholamines and SNS stimulation → increases HR
    • slope decreased with ACh or adenosine → decreases HR
