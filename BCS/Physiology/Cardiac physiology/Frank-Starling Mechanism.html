Frank-Starling Mechanism

Background

  • venous return into the heart affects the strength and thus its stroke volume
    • increasing venous return increases the filling pressure (also known as the End Diastolic Pressure; when referring to the systemic circulation, we describe the Left Ventricular End Diastolic Pressure, or LVEDP).
  • This response has a mechanism based on optimising sarcomere length
    • with more preload (LVEDP), the sarcomeres are stretched more optimally, allowing for stronger contractile strength
    • this is ultimately a local mechanism with nil extrinsic neurohumoral requirements
    • this is described by length-tension and force-veolicty relationships
      • increased preload = increased sarcomere stretch = active tension of muscle fibre = increased shortening velocity of fibre
      • increased sarcomere length = increased troponin C calcium sensitivity = increased cross bridge attachment/detachment = increased muscle fibre tension

Factors increasing stroke volume using the F-S mechanism

  • decreasing afterload
    • allows less resistance for the LV to push against
    • allows more blood to get through
    • therefore improved stroke volume
  • increased inotropic support
    • allows LV to generate more pressure
