Selection

- T-cell development and selection occurs in thymus - CD4+CD8+ (double positive) T-cells presented with self-antigens in thymus by cortical thymic epithelial cells - if presented via MHC-I → loses CD4 and becomes CD8+ - if presented via MHC-II → loses CD8 and becomes CD4+ - facilitated by AIRE - most cells dont get presented anything → die by neglect - low affinity binding → survive (positive selection) - high affinity binding → die (negative selection) - intermediate affinity → Tregs - mutations in AIRE results in failure of central tolerance - autoimmune polyendocrinopathy Syndrome-1 (APS1 or APECED) - results in highly reactive cells in system → autoimmunity - can develop anticytokine antibodies which can result in chronic mucocutaneous candidiasis

Antigen presentatino

- dendritic cell encounters microbe in tissue - takes up antigen, gets to lymph node - presents to lymphocyte - when presented via - MHC-II → speaks to CD4+ T-cell → found in innate immune cells, and antigen presenting cells - will present phagocystosed, exogenous proteins - if MHC-I → speaks to CD8+ T-cell → found in all nucleated cells - presents endogenous antigens encoded by host or viral genome - cross-presentation can occur whereby exogenous proteins are presented via MHC-I on dendritic cells - MHC-TCR interaction is called the immunological synapse

Cytotoxic lymphocytes

- e.g. in case of virally infected macrophage - granzymes and perforins released - FAS release w/ FAS-L can activate 'death receptors - cytokines for inflammation and inducing cell death - if not efficient - defective apoptosis occurs → chronically infected macrophages releasing cytokines and cytotoxic lymphocyhtes releasing cytokines → profound inflammatory response → hemophagocytosis - e.g. perforin deficiency, granzyme vesicle trafficking difficulty → will result in arrested apoptosis

T-cell differentiation

- Needs 3 signals 1. Antigen presentatino with naive lymphocyte 2. Cytokine milieu - dictated by innate immune response - different TLR activation will release different cytokines - e.g. TLR2 → IL-12 and IFN release → signal Stat4/Tbet transcription → produces Th1 cell and itself releasing IFNy and TNF - Th1 important in macrophage activation and killing intracellular organisms - e.g. IL4 release → Stat6/GATA3 response → Th2 cell induction → IL4, IL5, IL13 release - Th2 is to induce helminth responses and allergies 1. Co-stimulation (at immunologic synapse) - CD80-CD28 binding (CD28 CDb7.1/2) - regulators need to stop it from going AWOL - CTLA4 - transported to surface of Treg → binds to CD80/86 which stops it from binding CD21 → stops lymphocyte activation - can be dysregulated by tumours - absence can occur through CTLA haploinsufficiency (gene mutation, loss of function) or LRBA deficiency (important for trafficking CTLA4 to surface) - can result in autoimmunity and immunodeficiency - - complication of melanoma treatment with CTLA4 → autoimmunity - PD1 - inhibits TCR signalling, leads to T-cell anergy, inhibits cell growth, inhibits T-cell effector functino, targeting antibodies 'awaken' anergic cells - - targeting PD1 in immunotherapy awakens anergic cells

B-cell immunity

- B-cell maturation occurs in bone marrow - immature B-cells released into periphery as IgM and IgD positive lymphocyte - encounters antigen → produces more IgM - only when interacting with follicular T-cell can it class switch - class switch recombination depends on cytokines the T-cell is producing - CD40 and CD40L interactions occuring at B/T-cell synapse is important - absence of T-cell results in hyperIgM syndrome

Rethinking primary immunodeficiency

  • Immune deficiency is not just about infections
    • 50% have non-infectious complications → makes diagnosis hard
    • autoimmunity, inflammation, malignancy, allergy and infections may be related and result in immune deficiency
  • ESID cohort (n=2700)
    • bronchiectasis, AIHA, ITP, lymphoma, GI disturbances more common in CVID vs. general population
    • autoimmunity can precede infection
    • can have >1 condition - think twice when patient presents with more than 1 autoimmune condition
    • autoimmunity risk higher in CVID patients
    • poor prognosis and shorter survival

Cytokine signalling

- cytokine bind to cytokine receptor - subunits may be shared amongst multiple cytokines - JAK/STAT pathway → STAT translocation into nucleus and transcription occurs - Different cytokines signal through different JAKs and STATs - some signal through the same process - e.g. IL23 and IL12 both affected by same pathways

- Defects can affect JAKs or STATs - LOF or GOF defects have implications - JAK GOF → Haematological malignancies and myelofibrosis picture - STAT GOF - e.g. STAT1 GOF → autoimmunity, chronic mucutaneous candidaisis, cerebral aneursyms - LOF → myobacterial and viral infections - more new JAK inhibitors (JAKinibs)
