Muscle anatomy

Function

  • shortening of muscle allows for movement
  • shortening occurs in response to stimuli
    • e.g. electric shock, nerve impulse
  • supplied by
    • nerves, blood, lymphatics

Components of muscle

  1. Muscle belly
  2. Attachments
    1. Tendons
      1. rounded attachments
    2. Aponeuroses
      1. flat sheet attachments

Types of muscle

  1. Skeletal muscle
    1. Found on skeleton
      1. moves joints of the skeletal system
    2. Striated muscle
    3. Suppled by
      1. somatic nerves - i.e. voluntary control
  2. Cardiac muscle
    1. Found in the heart only
      1. contraction of heart wall muscles
    2. Striated muscle
    3. Supplied by
      1. Autonomic nerves - i.e. INvoluntary control
  3. Smooth muscle
    1. Found in the walls of hollow organs, blood vessels, eyes, skin
      1. regulates organ size, movement, etc.
    2. Non-striated / smooth muscle
    3. Supplied by
      1. Autonomic nerves - i.e. INvoluntary control

Shapes of muscle

  1. Flat muscle (with aponeuroses)
  2. Uni- bi- and multi-pennate muscles
  3. Fusiform (e.g. biceps)
  4. Quadrate (e.g. rhomboids)
  5. Circular (e.g. sphincter)
