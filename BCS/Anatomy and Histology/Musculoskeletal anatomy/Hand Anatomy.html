Hand Anatomy

  • Orientation

    • radial vs ulnar
    • distal vs. proximal
    • dorsal vs. palmar

  • Skeleton

    • bones and joints - 27 bones, 19 long bones (form 5 days)
      • 14 phalanges, 2 sesamoids, 5 metacarpals, 8 carpals, supported by radius and ulna
    • carpal bones
      • proximal row (Radial to ulnar)
        • scaphoid - 'boat' shaped bone articulates proximally within the radius and has a proximal tubercle. Scaphoid fractures are prominent, difficult to diagnose and treat
        • lunate "moon" shaped bone articulates proximally with radius. it is the home of the Kineboeck's disease (vascular necrosis condition)
        • triquetrum - "three cornered" bone articulates proximally with triangular fibrocartilage of distal radioulnar joint
        • pisiform - "pea" bone lines on palmar surface of triquetrum. Technically a sesamoid bone
      • distal row (R to U)
        • Trapezium - "table" bone articulates with 1st metacarpal bone (1st CMC jt). Important for thumb function
        • trapezoid - "little table" bone. Small wedge shaped bone. Articulates distally with 2nd metacarpal bone.
        • Capitate - "head" bone. large bone in centre on the wrist. Articulates distally with 3rd metacarpal bone base
        • Hamate - wedge shaped and has a hooked process "hook of hamate" - hook and curve of hook form the radial border of Guyons tunnel with ulnar nerve and artery
    • Metacarpal bones and joints
      • numbered 1 to 5; proximal end are bases, distal ends are heads
      • MCP joints, CMC joints
    • Phalanges
      • thumb has interphalangeal joint (no DIP or PIP jts)
  • Soft tissue
    • muscles and tendons - move bones and joints
      • Extrinsic hand muscles
        • Flexors - medial epicondyle
        • Extensions - lateral epicondyle
      • Intrinsic hand muscles
        • thenar and hypothenar muscles
        • 7 interossei (4 dorsai, 3 volar)
        • 4 lumbrical muscles
      • Flexion of the finger
        • Flexor digitalis profundis (FDP) and flexor digitalis superficialis (FDS) pass through fibro-osseous tunnels lined with synovia
        • thumb has long flexor in forearm, short flexor in thenar group
          • abductor, adductor, opponens muscles for thumb
      • Extensors of funger
        • 6 compartments at dorsum of wrist to extensor appartus of each finger
  • nerves - innervates muscle
    • median nerve
      • sensation to palmar/radial aspect of hand
      • innervation of radial extrinsic flexors and intrinsic thenar muscles + lumbricals 1 and 2
      • enters hand through carpal tunnel - at risk of carpal tunnel syndrome
    • ulnar nerve
      • sensation of ulnar hand
      • innervation of ulnar extrinsic flexors, intrinsic interossei and hypothenar muscles (via deep motor branch), lumbrical muscles 3 + 4, abductor pollicus
      • enters hand through guyons canal
    • radial nerve
      • sensation to radial/dorsal hand
      • innervation of extensor muscles of digits and wrist and abductor pollicus longus muscle
  • vessels
    • Radial/ulnar artery form superficial and deep vascular arches of hand
      • test circulation with Allen's test
        • exsanguinate hand by elevation, pump blood out of hand
        • occlude radial and ulnar arteries at wrist
        • release one of the arteries to test blood flow (reperfusion)
          • could indicate venous obstruction
      • venous drainage (Superficial and deep veins)
        • superficial veins are large and on dorsum of hand, sitting on fascia
        • deep venous system accompanies arterial system
    • lymphatic drainage
      • accompany veins of hand and wrist
      • epitrochlear, axillary, delto-pectoral lymph nodes
  • skin and fascia
    • thick mobile elastic skin on dorsum of hand
    • thick rigid skin in palm of hand
    • palmar fascia/aponeurosis as stabilising and protecting layer
      • important for fascial tunneling for neurovascular structures
