Bone histology

Background

  • Living material composed of cells and extracellular matrix
  • ECM:
    • inorganic phase - 60% dry weight, made of Calcium hydroxyapatite
    • organic phase - 40% dry weight, majority
      • Majority Type 1 collagen (bONE)
        • Chains of hydroxyproline
        • triple helix composed of 3 interwoven protein alpha chains
        • strong and very flexible - provides tensile strength
      • Proteoglycans - provide compressive force
      • Matrix proteins (Osteoglycans)
    • aqueous phase

Cells in the bone

Osteoprogenitor cells

  • Osteoprogenitor cells - stem cells which become bone cells

Osteoblast

  • Osteoblasts - immature cells which form bone
    • From mesenchymal stem cells
    • Runx-2/cbfa-1 transcription factors drive mesenchymal stem cells to differentiate to osteoblasts
      • deficiency of runx-2/cbfa-1 results in Cleidocranial dysplasia
    • cuboid cells aligned in layers along immature osteoid - forms osteoid
    • functions
      • forms bone
      • regulates osteoclasts
      • regulates bone metabolism
    • secretion of type I collagen
    • Markers: ALP, osteocalcium, osteonectin, osteopontin
    • Receptors
      • PTH - stimulated by pulsed PTH
        • tereparatide used to stimulate this in osteoporosis
      • Wnt
        • binds LRP5/6 to stimulate osteoblast survival and proliferation
        • deficiency → osteopenia
        • excess → high bone mass
        • sequestered by sclerostin (Scl) and dickkopf-related protein 1 (dkk1)
      • Sclerostin (Scl)
        • sequersters Wnt, secreted by osteocytes
        • negative feedback on osteoblasts - can inhibit bone formation
        • sclerostin antibody may improve bone density and improve fracture repair
    • Produces
      • RANK Ligand produced by osteoblast
      • Osteoprotegrin

Osteocytes

  • Osteocytes - maintain bone
    • 90% of cells in mature skeleton
    • former osteoblasts trapped in matrix
    • function
      • maintains bone
      • mechanosensing and control extracellular concentrations of calcium and phosphorus
    • receptors
      • calcitonin - stimulation
      • PTH - inhibition
    • communication via canaliculi in bone

Osteoclasts

  • Osteoclasts - different lineage, absorb bone
    • multi-nucleated bone resorbing cell from a monocyte lineage
    • resorbs mineralised bone matrix in Howship's lacunae
    • Structure
      • ruggled border
      • bind via integrins
      • Carbonic anhydrase
        • produce H+ to dissolve mineralised bone matrix into Ca2+, H3PO4, H2CO3 (inorganic phase)
      • Cathepsin-K
        • lysosomal enzyme dissolving proteins/collagen (organic phase of bone)
    • differentiation
      • RANK-L
      • Macrophage colony stimulating hormone
    • Receptors
      • PTH - continuous PTH stimulates osteoclasts
      • Estrogen - inhibition of osteoclasts by estrogen
      • RANK receptor - stimulated by RANK-L (from osteoblasts)
