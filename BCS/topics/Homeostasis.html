Homeostasis

Background

Heomstoastasis is the maintenance of nearly constant conditions in the internal environment (Walter Cannon).

Examples of homeostasis

  1. Your body controls the Na+ ion concentration within your blood to a variation of a few millimoles per litre
  2. Capillaries are located extremely close to cells (few are > 50micrometres away from a capillary) - this close apposition allows rapid transport of nutrients and removal of waste products from cells
  3. Regulation of O2 and CO2 in ECF
  4. Regulation of Arterial BP

Examples of breakdown in homeostasis

  1. Diseases which impair kidney salt excretion → raised BP (which increases eGFR and allows ongoing excretion) → prolonged HTN can result in kidney injury → higher BP → more kidney injury (positive feedback mechanism)
