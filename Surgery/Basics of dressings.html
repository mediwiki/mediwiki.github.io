Dressings

Dressing types

  1. Moisturising
    • Hydrogel - a jelly-like material (typically made of glycerine) containing water in a polymer matrix
      • can provide and diffuse out H2O into wound
      • creates a moist environment if the wound is dry
      • creates a cooling pain/symptom relief
  2. Absorbent
    • Alginate - sheet of felt-like material with algae polymers
    • alginate + CMC - alginate and cellulose-based material
      • CMC can absorb H2O
    • Foam - porous material
      • draws water up like a sponge
  3. Moisturising/absorbent
    • Hydrocolloid dressing - hydrocolloid attached to thin polyurethane film
      • absorbs moisture whilst taking bacteria from the surface and absorbs bacteria onto surface
      • when removed, acts as mechanical debridement
  4. Protective
    • low-adhesive dressings
      • looks like gauze; large strands which don't really allow moisture absorbent, etc
      • only real purpose is to prevent further abrasion to the surface
  5. Special dressings
    • odor control dressings
      • pad with activated charcoal
    • antimicrobial dressings
      • iodine/iodoform-based dressings
      • silver-based dressings
