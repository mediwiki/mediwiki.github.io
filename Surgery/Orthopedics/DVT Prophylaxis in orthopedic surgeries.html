Background

  • DVT prophylaxis in orthopedic surgeries are necessary given the high risk of DVT formation
    • See Virchow's triad

General rules

  • You should assess risk of bleeding vs. VTE risk prior to starting
  • VTEp should go on for 6 weeks whilst a limb is immobilised
  • VTEp with enoxaparin, fondaparinux, apixaban and dabigatran considered off-label to young people under 18
  • VTEp with aspirin is considered off label

Scenarios

Lower limb immobilisation

  • VTEp with LMWH or fondaparinux up to 6 weeks

Fragility fractures of lower limb (hip, knee, NOF)

  • Start LWMH 6-12 hours post-operatively OR fondaparinux 6 house post-operatively
  • Give VTEp prior to surgery if surgery is delayed
    • Last dose LWMH 12 hours prior to OT
    • Last dose fondaparinux 24h prior to OT
  • Consider mechanical DVTp if pharmacological prophylaxis contraindicated
  • Continue until patient no longer has significatly reduced mobility

Elective hip

  • VTEp with any of the following regimens
    1. LMWH for 10 days, aspirin (75mg ot 150mg) for further 28 days
    2. LMWH for 28 years with antiembolic stockings (until discharge)
    3. Rivaroxaban 10mg OD (marketing guidance)
    4. Apixaban 2.5mg BD (marketing guidance)
    5. Dabigatran (150mg or 220mg OD) starting 1-4 hours post-operatively
    6. Anti-embolic

Elective knee

  • VTEp with any of the following regimens
    • Aspirin (75 or 150mg) OD for 14 days
    • LWMH 14 days with antiembolic stockings til discharge
    • Rivaroxaban
    • Apixaban
    • Dabigatran
    • Mechanical DVTp

Non-elective knee replacement

  • DVTp generally not needed where
    • total anaesthesia time < 90 minutes
    • low risk of DVTp
  • If DVTp is needed
    • LWMH 6-12h post-op for 14 days

Foot and ankle surgery

  • DVTp should be considered where
    • immobilisation occurs > 42 days
    • total anesthesia time > 90 minutes
    • VTE risk outweights risk of bleeding

Upper limb surgery

  • VTEp not needed if giving local or regional anaesthetic for upper limb surgery
  • considered DVTp where
    • total time under GA > 90 minutes
    • operation will make it difficult for patient to mobilise
