Acute Knee Pain

Differentials

  1. Inflammatory
    • Rheumatoid arthritis
    • Crystal arthropathy
    • Bursitis
    • Tendonitis
  2. Iatrogenic
    1. Recent knee injections (postinjection inflammation)
  3. Infective
    • Acute Osteomyelitis - fevers, constitutional symptoms (in context of immunocompromise - HIV, sickle cell, diabetes, alcoholism, corticosteroid use), IVDU
    • Septic arthritis - fever, erythema, irritable joint
  4. Vascular
    • Haemarthrosis
    • Bone infarct - Hx of hemophilia or sickle cell
  5. Neoplastic
    • Pathological fracture - insidious onset, constitutional symptoms
  6. Traumatic
    • Fracture - inability to bear weight
    • Tendon or ligament injury
    • Knee tendon rupture - patellar/quadriceps - unable to actively and fully extend knee

History

  • Ask about constitutional symptoms - traumas can occur even in settings of pathological fractures
  • Ask about previous surgeries - hardware can serve as nidus for infection
    • Recent surgery (< 3 months) are at higher risk for virulent organisms (E.g. S. aureus)
    • Delayed onset (> 6 months post-operative) are more likely less virulent organisms (e.g S. epidermidis)
  • Recurrent fractures - increased suspicion for infection (e.g. Osteomyelitis) and neoplasms (e.g. Osteosarcoma)
  • Weight bearing
    • Fractures and

Investigations

  1. Gait
  2. Look
    • Expose and compare both knees
    • Atrophy, scarring, swelling, bowing, landmarks
  3. Feel
    • Temperature
    • Effusion
    • Joint line tenderness - medial/lateral meniscus injuries or arthritis
    • Neurovascular exam (pulses, sensation)
      • Pulses must be assessed and ABI performed if uncertainty (ABI < 0.9 indicates high likelihood of vascular injury)
      • Foot drop and dorsal numbness suggests Common peroneal nerve injury
  4. Move (passive)
    • Range of motion
    • Crepitus
    • McMurray sign → Meniscal cartilage
    • Lachman's test → ACL
    • Posterior drawer test
    • Pivotal shift test
    • Dial test
    • Varus/valgus instability
    • Locking and catching - indicates a mechanical blockage to motion (e.g. tear, mobile flaps, loose osteochondral bodies)
    • Passive stretch test
