Rapid orthopedic exam

Basics of extremity injuries

  • joint above and below
  • sensory
  • motor +/- reflexes
  • vascular
  • skin
    • open fracture, road rash, pressure ulcer risk
  • compartments

Joint above and below

  • Look at joints above and below as they can be affected
    • e.g. in FOOSH, you have
      • Monteggia - MU (monteggia, ulnar proximal)
      • Galleazi - GR (Galliazi, Radius distal)

Rapid hand exam

  • Name the fingers - thumb, index, middle, ring, pinky
  • Dorsal and volar
  • Ulnar and Radial
  • Snuff box
  • Elbow vs wrist = JA
  • Anatomic description
    • proximal/distal to which joint
  • Detail at injury
  • Say what it isn't
  • Nerve functions
    • median nerve
      • pure motor nerve - recurrent median nerrve - common FOOSH injury
        • the british nerve (apposition, flex at wrist, pronates, elbow flexion) → think drinking tea
    • radial nerve - STOP
      • pure sensory nerve - superficial branch of radial nerve - risk of injury in knife injury
        • sensory to back of hand
    • Ulnar - interosseouss muscles

- Flexors - FDS - stops at PIP - FDP - stops DIP

Missed ligamentous injuries

  • Scapholunate ligament injury
    • Terry Thomas sign
    • gap between scaphoid and lunate in carpal XR
  • Ski-pole thumb
    • injury to ulnar collateral ligament
    • avulsion in PIP thumb
