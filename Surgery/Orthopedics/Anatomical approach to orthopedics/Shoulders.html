Anatomy and function

  • Sternoclavicular joint only synovial joint between upper limb and axial skeleton
  • glenohumeral joint closely controlled by deltoid and rotate cuff muscles (subscapularis, supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor)

Congenital abnormalities

Sprengel's Shoulder

Acquired abnormalities

Fractures

Clavicle fractures

Approach

  • History
    • unusual event (Trauma, excess activity)
    • Onset (sudden vs. gradual)
    • Age, Occupation
    • History of neck problems
  • Examination
    • if localisable pain - unlikely referred from neck
    • Inflammation/impingement:
      • Hawkin's test for impingement
    • Tears
    • Instability
      • Apprehension test
  • Investigations
    • XR shoulder - limited value as injuries tend to be soft tissue
      • reduced subacromial space - full thickness rotator cuff tear
      • subacromial spur - calcification of coracoacromial ligament - ?degenerative cuff disease
    • UItrasound/MRI
      • for integrity/health of rotate cuff
      • MR arthrography - labrum integrity of glenohumeral joint

  1. Brief didactic teaching / group discussion session (15-20 mins)

  2. Revise bony, ligamentous and muscular anatomy of the shoulder and Rotator cuff injury

  3. History and examination

  4. Shoulder pain history if appropriate patient discovered

  5. Otherwise facilitate multiple members of the group performing a formal shoulder examination
