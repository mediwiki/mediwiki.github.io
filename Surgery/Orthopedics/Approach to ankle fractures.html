Ottawa ankle and foot rules for X-ray scans

X- rays are only indicated if:

  • 4 steps at ED AND 4 steps at presentation
  • Base of 5th metatarsal tenderness OR navicular bone pain
  • 6 cm posterosuperior to the medial OR the lateral malleolus

Physical exams

  • Assessment of gait
    • Assesses ability to bear weight
    • Often will have antalgic gait
  • Anterior Drawer test
    • assesses integrity of anterior talofibular ligament
    • Patient sits with knee flexed to relax gastrocnemius-soleus complex + plantarflex ankle by 10º
    • Hold heel and forcefully pull forward with one hand while stabilising the proximal leg
    • Severe anterior subluxation = damage to this ligament
  • Inversion stress test/talar tilt test
    • assesses integrity of calcaneofibular ligament
    • Patient has knee flexed, grab heel and tilt talus into varus (inversion)
    • Tilt > 23º suggests damage to this ligament
  • Squeeze/Hopkin’s test
    • compress fibula/tibia in midcalf
    • Positive = pain elicited over syndesmosis = injury to syndesmosis
  • External rotation test
    • stabilise leg proximal to ankle joint while grasping plantar aspect of food and externally rotating it
    • Pain = injury to syndesmosis
  • Note on physical examination
    • often pain in the acute setting precludes assessment
    • Repeat clinical examination is indicated once pain and swelling have subsided - generally after 2 weeks

Fractures

  • Inversion (supination) of ankle can cause fibular fractures
    • Weber A - fracture distal to syndesmosis
    • Weber B - Level of syndesmosis
    • Weber C - proximal to syndesmosis
  • Calcaneus fractures
    • Anterior process of calcaneus fracture → lateral malleolar bony tenderness
  • Base of 5th metatarsal fracture
    • Inversion injury
    • Pain on lateral border of foot, especially on weight bearing
      • Zone 1 fracture - avulsion injury from proximal tubercle - heals well
      • Zone 2 fracture - meta-diaphyseal junction - in a vascular watershed so often doesn’t heal well (prone to non-union)
      • Zone 3 fracture - diaphyseal - also high rate of non-union
