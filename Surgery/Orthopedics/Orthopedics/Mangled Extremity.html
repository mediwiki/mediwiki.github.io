Mangled extremity

Definition

In the case of an orthopedic emergency secondary to trauma, compartments with fractured bones should be assessed for:

  1. Nerves
  2. Bloods vessels
  3. Bones
  4. Soft tissues

Injury to >3/4 of these components consistitutes a 'mangled extremity'

Evaluation - Mangled Extremity Severity Score

Management

  • Factors favouring amputation
    • hemodynamically unstable
    • prolonged ischemia
    • severity of force (degree of mangling)
    • faster return to function but higher long term costs due to prosthesis
  • Limb salvage
    • Risk of prolonged hospitalization, multiple surgeries, high initial cost (and psychological toll) however typically more preferrable
