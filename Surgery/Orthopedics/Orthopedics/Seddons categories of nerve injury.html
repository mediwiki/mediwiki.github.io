Seddon's 3 basic categories of nerve injury

Background

  • See Nerve Sheath

Categories

  1. Neuropraxia
    • Myelin injury but no axon/nerve sheath injury
    • Results in temporary nerve conduction block and loss of motor/sensory function but NOT autonomic
    • Fully recivery expected within hours to months
  2. Axonotmesis
    • Myelin and axon injury with in tact nerve sheath
    • Wallerian degeneration occurs with motor/sensory AND autonomic paralysis
      • Degeneration can occur at approx 1mm/day, and affected by age and nutritional status
    • Incomplete recovery within weeks to months - in tact nerve sheath means some axon regeneration
  3. Neurotmesis
    • Myelon, axon AND nerve sheath injury
    • Recovery is variable but complete, usually requiring surgery
    • can result in permanent paralysis

Wallerian degeneration

  • Axonal degenation/injury
  • Occurs with axonotmesis and neurotmesis but NOT neurpraxia

Sunderland's classification of neurotmesis

  1. Endoneurium alone affected
  2. Endoneurium AND perineurium affected
  3. All three (endoneurium, perineurium, epineurium) affected - i.e. complete transection/avulsion
