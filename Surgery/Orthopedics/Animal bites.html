Animal bites

Background

  • Bite wounds
    • Domestic/wild animals
    • Humans

Pathology

  • Bacterial infectious
    • Environmental bacteria
    • Oral flora of biter
    • Skin flora

Clinical features

Diagnostic criteria

  • Patient history
    • Ascertain type of animal, provoked/unprovoked, situation/environment
    • Antimicrobial allergies, current medications, splenectomy, mastectomy, liver disease, immunosuppression
  • Examination
    • Type/depth/location of injury
    • Range of motion / nerve / tendon function
    • infective signs
    • exudate

Investigations

  • Swab and cultures of bite
  • XR - for ?bony injury and baseline for ?OM evalulation

Management

  1. Irrigate wound with 0.9% sodium chloride (copious amounts)
  2. Immunosation
    1. Rabies
    2. Tetanus
  3. surgical debridement of devitalised or necrotic tissue
    • typically not for primary wound closure
    • allow delayed primary closure or allow closure by secondary intention
    • loosely approximate wound edges with adhesive strip/sutures for selected fresh and uninfected wounds
  4. Antibiotic coverage
    • Consider Pasturella, Streptococcus, Staphylococcus aureus and anerobes (incl. Fusobacterium, Porphyromonas, Prevotella, Bacteroides)
    • Oral antimicrobial choices
      • Amox/clav 875/125 BD
      • Clindamycin 300mg QID + ciprofloxacin 500mg BD
      • Clindamycin 300mg QID + TMP/SMX double strength BD
      • Moxifloxacin 400mg OD
      • Doxyclcyline 100mg BD
    • IV antibiotic choices
      • Ampicillin/sulbactam 1.5-3g IV q6hrly
      • Cefoxitin 1-2g IV q6-8hrly
  5. In-hospital management
    • Monitor for toxicity/infection
    • Elevation
    • Immobilisation (consider splint)
    • Report to LHDs
