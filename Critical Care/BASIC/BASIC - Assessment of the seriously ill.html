Model of medical treatment

  1. History
  2. Examination
  3. Investigation
  4. Diagnosis

  5. Treatment

  6. Model does not work when there is not much time - especially in seriously ill patients

Rapid assessment

  1. Airway
    1. Stridor absent on airway obstruction, esp in severe cases
    2. normal SpO2 does not exclude compromised airway
    3. Hypercarbia and reduced LOC -> compensatory mechanisms are exhausted
    4. bradycardia + airway obstruction -> impending cardiorespiratory arrest
    5. assess SNS response - as a magnitude response to severeity or illness
  2. Breathing
    1. Tachypnoea -> good marker of severely ill
    2. Pulse Oximetry - significant desaturation is late feature of inadequate ventilation
    3. Tachypnoea with nil respiratory failure -> metabolic acidosis or sepsis
    4. Low RR -> ?respiratory arrest impending
    5. Assess SNS response
    6. Worry if
      1. FF > 30/min (or <8/min)
      2. Unable to speak 1/2 sentences without pausing
      3. agitation, confusion, comatose
      4. cyanosis or SpO2 < 90%
      5. deterioration despite therapy
  3. Circulation
    1. Hypotension is late shock feature
    2. Assess tissue perfusion - consciousness, peripheral circulation, Urine Output, acidosis, appearance
      • E.g. look for cyclical mask condensation -> patient is breathing
    3. IF in shock, assess causes of shock - HR, JVP, peripheral temperature
    4. Ask about physiological (esp cardiopulmonary) reserve
      1. exercise tolerance - how far, fast, and why s/he stops walking
      2. previous major illnesses
  4. Investigations
    • Depends on clinical presentation
    • Routine investigations: FBC, EUC, CMP, LFT, INR, ABG, BGL, CXR
  5. Treatment
    1. need to know what treatment the patient is already receiving
  6. Later
    1. comprehensive history
    2. Examination, severity of physiological insult

Resuscitation approach when there is no time

  1. Start resuscitation based on probable cause
  2. take history whilst resuscitating
  3. start investigations throughout treatment

Important considerations

  • do NOT leave the patient until they are stable
  • do things in parallel

Hypotension

  • differs depending on usual BP for patient
  • All non-pregnant non-anaesthetised adults with SBP < 90mmHg should be considered seriously ill
    • a few will have no other signs of shock but should tread with caution

- SNS response and homeostatic response - failure of compensation = severe disease
