Definition

  • Acute Respiratory Failure - Pulmonary system not meeting metabolic demands of body
    1. Hypoxaemic respiratory failure
      • PaO2 < 60mmHg when on RA
    2. Hypercapnic respiratory failure
      • PaCO2 >= 50mmHg

Basic respiratory physiology

  1. Function of lung - O2 in and CO2 out
  2. Oxygen in dependencies:
    • Alveolar O2 partial pressure (PAO2)
      • Alveolar pressure is the sum of all the pressures within the alveoli, comprising of O2, CO2, H2O and N2
      • increasing alveolar pressure will increase all constituent partial pressures
      • increasing alveolar O2 will proportionally decrease PP of all other gases
      • increasing alveolar CO2 (e.g. in hypoventilation) will proportionally decrease PP of all other gases (mainly O2)
      • Therefore PAO2 depends on the
        • FiO2, Alveolar pressure, PACO2 and ventilation
    • diffusing capacity of alveolar membrane
    • ventilation
      • CO2 removal is dependent on alveolar ventilation
      • Alveolar Ventilation (CO2 out) = RR x (Vt - Vd)
        • RR = respiratory rate
        • Vt = tidal volume
        • Vd = dead space - varies depending on V/Q matching
    • perfusion
    • ventilation-perfusion matching
      • perfusion of nonventilated lung = shunting - this is the most common cause of hypoxaemic respiratory failure in CO2
