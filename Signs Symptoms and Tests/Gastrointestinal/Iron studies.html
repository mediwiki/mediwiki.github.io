Iron studies

Components

  • Ferritin
    • intracellular iron stores
    • released into the serum in small amounts normally
    • in inflammation (liver disease, malignancy, etc.)
      • cells cannot hang onto their ferritin, and is released in inflammatory cascades
      • ?release of ferritin allows other cells in need to capture ferritin and use it
    • acute phase reactant - elevates in inflammation
  • Serum iron
    • Fe3+ bound to serum transferrin
    • highly variable, affected by dietary iron intake, inflammation, infection
  • Transferrin
    • Iron transport protein in plasma
    • its number is a reflection of how much iron is needed
      • high requirement for iron (e.g. iron deficiency) = high transferrin
  • Transferrin saturation
    • Typically transferrin is 30% saturated with iron in majority
    • iron overload = transferrin will be oversaturated (Where else would the iron go?)
    • iron deficiency = transferrin will be undersaturated (transferrin increases due to increased demand - above; however not enough iron to saturate)
    • transient increases in TF saturations can occur with rapid increased dietary intake of iron
      • isolated transferrin elevation - repeat on fasting sample to eliminate dietary component
      • can also rise following recent intake of iron replacement - wait ~ 4 weeks post cessation of replacement prior to assessing iron studies
  • Total iron bindinc capacity
    • availability of iron binding sites on transferrin
    • effectively the inverse of transferrin saturation

Interpretation

Iron overload

  • Causes
    • primary overload (e.g. Hereditary Haemochromatosis)
    • secondary overload (e.g. repeat blood transfusion, iron loading anaemias, iron supplements, Porphyria Cutanea Tarda)
  • persistently raised TF saturation â†’ HFE gene analysis and pre-test counselling
  • pan-elevated iron studies can occur in acute hepatic injury due to intracellular leakage - can give impression of iron overload

Iron deficiency

  • consider measuring CRP if serum ferritin normal/high and serum iron/TFsat low
    • as serum ferritin normally used to diagnose iron deficiency
    • normal/high serum ferritin can be a false marker given elevation as an acute phase reactant
