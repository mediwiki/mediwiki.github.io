First trimester ultrasound

Use

  • Antenatal care
    • 6 week dating scan - estimate due date
    • 11-14 week nuchal translucency - chromosomal abnormality
    • 18-20 week morphology scan
    • 3rd trimester growth and wellbeing scan
  • only becomes useful after 4th week of pregnancy
    • gestational sac seen
    • intradecidual sac sign - collection or gestational sac seen on one side of uterine cavity
      • double decidual sac sign - two concentric rings, surrounding anechogenic gestational sac
    • absence of these signs does NOT exclude intrauterine pregnancy
  • 5-7 weeks
    • yolk sac on ultrasound
    • foetal pole - can measure crown-rump length
    • foetal heart rate can be detected to establish viability of pregnancy
      • should be > 100bpm
  • 7-8 weeks
    • cephalad and caudal poles visible
    • limb buds visible > 8 weeks
    • some visible foetal movement possible

Images

- 5-7 week: yolk sac - 7-8 week, foetal poles

Pathology

  • Perigestational haemorrhage
    • frequent cause of 1st and 2nd trimester bleed
    • small - <20% gestational sac surrounded
    • large - >50% gestational sac surrounded
  • Twin pregnancy - 15% of all pregnancies
    • increased likelihood with IVF
    • Dizigotic twins 70% > monozygotic twins 30%
    • increased foetal/neonatal mortality (3-6x) with twin pregnancies
    • Dichorionic twins
      • 2 gestational sacs
      • 2 placental masses
      • 2 yolk sacs
      • inter-twin membrane in continuity with placenta - 'twin peak' and 'lambda' sign on ultrasound
    • monochorionic twins
      • small gestational sac
      • single placental mass
      • 2 yolk sacs
      • thin inter-twin membrane - 'T' sign
    • Monochorionic monoamniotic twins
      • small gestational sac
      • single placental mass
      • usually 1 yolk sac
      • absent inter-twin membrane - entangled cords can occur
  • Molar pregnancy - gestational trophoblastic disease w/ chorionic villi in uterine mass
    • complete mole
      • absence of foetus, solid mass with numerous small (3-10mm) anechoic spaces - 'bunch of grapes sign'
    • partial mole - abnormal foetus, not viable with life
      • enlarged placenta with multiple anechoic lesions
  • Ectopic pregnancy - fertilised ovum implants in somewhere other than the uterus
    • 1-2% of pregnancy, increases with IVF
    • can be life threatening and associated with haemorrhage
    • Location
      • tubal - 93-97%
      • Ovarian - 0.5-1%
      • Cervix - 1%
      • Scar from previous caesarian
      • Abdominal - 1.4%
    • Specific sign in US - live extrauterine pregnancy (though rare to find)
    • Common but less specific signs
      • Free intraperitoneal fluid
      • Complex adnexal cyst/mass
      • 'Tubal' sign or 'Ring of fire' sign - echogenic ring surrounding unruptured ectopic pregnancy, indicates hypervascular lesion with peripheral vascularity
        • note this is the same named sign as that of the corpus luteum

- dichorionic twins, lambda/twin peak sign - monochorionic twins, T sign - monochorionic monoamniotic twins - complete mole, 'bunch of grapes' sign, transvaginal ultrasound - partial mole

- ectopic; ring of fire sign
