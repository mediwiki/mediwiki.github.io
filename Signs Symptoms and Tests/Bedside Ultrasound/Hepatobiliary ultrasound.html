Hepatobiliary ultrasound

Method

  • Curvilinear 2-5MHz tranducer
  • Abdomen preset
  • Supine position OR right-roll and left oblique or lateral decubitus
  • Right anterior oblique/lateral decubitus
    • Right subcostal margin in sagittal plane
    • inspire to enable liver dome visualisation

Normal anatomy - blood supply

  • Dual blood supply
    1. Portal vein - from confluence of splenic vein and SMV
    2. hepatic artery - aorta via coeliac trunk
  • venous drainage
    1. Right hepatic vein
    2. Middle hepatic vein
    3. Left hepatic vein
    4. converge radially in the superior of the liver

Normal anatomy - liver segments

  • 8 functional segments
    • hepatic veins at periphery of each segment
    • portal vein, hepatic arteries and bile ducts course through centre
    • Segment 1 - segment 1
    • Lateral (2+3), medial (4) in left lobe
    • Anterior (5+8) and posterior (6+7) in right lobe

Normal anatomy - echogenicity

  • homogenoeus and moderately echogenic
    • less echogenic than spleen and pancreas
    • more echogenic/isoechoic with right kidney
    • fatty infiltrate INCREASES echogenicity compared to right kidney
  • 'Parents So Love Kids' - Pancreas, Spleen, Liver, Kidneys (most to least echogenic)
  • normal echogenic structures in liver
    • Falciform - peritoneal fold attaches liver to anterior body wall
    • Main lobar fissure - connects gallbladder neck to portal vein at porta hepatis
      • divides right and left lobar lobes

Normal anatomy - size

  • measure vertical size from thoracic diaphragm to inferior edge along right midclavicular line
    • <15cm is normal

Liver pathology - Trauma

  • 3-10% of all abdominal traumas
    • left common than splenic trauma
    • liver parenchyma and subcapsular lacerations → altered echogenicity (reduced)
    • free abdominal fluid accompanise laceration
  • Solid organ injury - discrete hypoerechoic alteration in liver parenchyma and diffuse heterogeneous pattern in spleen
    • disorganised echo pattern
  • Hematomas - parenchymal or subcapsular fluid collections with changing echogenicity over time as hematoma organises
    • 24h - echogenic vs surrounding structures
    • 2-7 days - hypoechoic and distinct
    • 2-3 weeks - increasingly indistinct and blends with surrounding anatomy
  • Complications
    • hemoperitoneum - free fluid in abdomen
    • delayed pseudoanerythms in B-mode and doppler
    • Biloma - free fluid or extrahepatic loculated collection

Liver pathology - Steatosis

  • Mild infiltration - minimal diffuse increase in echogencity
  • Marked infiltration - marked increase in echogenicity and poor sound penetration
    • intrahepatic vessels and diaphragm blend with echogenic parenchyma
  • focal fat infiltration/sparing. changes over time
    • steatosis has no mass effect - regional vessels and contours won't change
    • colour doppler can be used to track vessels - may be confused with other hepatic masses
    • focal fat sparing tend to occur at periportal and pericholecystic regions

Liver pathology - infections

  • cholangitis
    • wall thickness normal/increased

    • 1.5mm = abnormal

    • US shows debris within duct; bile duct may be dilated and measure > 7.5mm
  • hepatitis
    • US may appear normal
    • Acute hepatitis
      • may show diffuse decrease in echogenicity - intrahepatic vessels may be comparatively bright - 'starry night appearance'
      • periportal cuffing - increased brightness of portal triads
    • Chronic hepatitis
      • diffused increase in liver echogenicity; periportal cuffing appears LESS EVIDENT due to blending with parenchyma
  • abscess
    • anechoic or hypoechoic mass
    • gas within abscess appears highly echogenic
  • Complications - portal venous or hepatic venous thrombosis
    • B-mode shows echo within vein
    • colour doppler shows total/partial occlusion of flow

Liver pathology - incidental lesions

  • Incidental lesions represent
    • liver cyst
      • 2.5% of general population, to 7% of people > 80 years
      • anechoic thin wall, well demarcated lesions
      • posterior acoustic enhancement
    • hemangioma
      • 4% of population
      • small <3cm hyperechoic well circumscribed lesions
      • no posterior shadow or enhacement
      • colour doppler may show increased vascularity within lesion
    • hepatocellular carcinoma
    • fibronodular hyperplasia
    • adenoma
    • liver metastases - gallbladder, colon, stomach, pancreas, breast, lung
      • round, hypoechoic mass with 'bulls eye' or 'target' pattern → inner/outer portion is more echogenic
      • less common
        • echogenicity increases from hypervascularity
        • calcified (from mucinous adenocarcinoma)
        • cystic
        • diffusely infiltrative

Gallbladder - normal anatomy

  • Lies near main lobar fissue
    • inferior to middle hepatic vein
    • anterior to right kidney
  • Long axis - gallbaldder is pear-shaped anechoic
    • thin echogenic wall <3mm
  • transverse
    • round/oval shaped structure, anechoic with thin-echogenic wall <3mm

- normal pear-shaped gallbladder - long axis - normal gallbaldder - transverse

Normal CBD anatomy

  • anterolateral to hepatic artery at porta hepatis
  • structures should have similar diameter
  • should be <5mm in people <50 years but enlarges after cholecystectomy
  • Long axis
    • two echogenic parallel lines
  • Short axis
    • small round anechoic structure with reflective walls
  • CBD shows no flow on colour doppler

Biliary tree pathology

  • nonvisualisation of the gallbladder
    • cholecystectomy, physiological contraction, chronic colecystitis, or congenital anomaly (agenesis , ectopia)
  • Biliary dilatation
    • Congenital dilation, choledocholithiasis, cholangitis, neoplastic obstruction, extrinsic compression
    • CBD average 3-4mm but ranges between 5-12mm
      • after 50 years → add 1-5mm for each decade after 50 years to account for presbyductia
      • diameter should be correlated with symptoms and LFTs
        • if asymptomatic and normal LFTs + >5mm diameter has very low diagnostic yield
        • if symptomatic with normal LFTs and >5mm - 6% will have pathology
    • US is examination of choice for suspected biliary dilation except in elderly where presbyductia may mask neoplastic obstruction
      • MRI and MRCP are gold standard for CBD imaging
  • biliary obstruction
  • cholecystitis
    • distended gallbladder +/- gallstones
    • gallbladder thickening >3mm +/- mucosal sloughing
    • Pericholecystic fluid
    • Positive murphy's - pressure over gallbadder causing pain
    • Complications
      • gallbladder necrosis → abscess or haemorrhage
      • Gallstone ileus - can cause SBO
      • Emphysematous cholecystitis → acute infection of wall caused by gas forming organisms
      • Perforation → peritonitis - US can show pericholecystic fludi collection and possible wall defect
    • Other causes of thickened wall - but not requiring urgent surgical intervention
      • chronic cholecystitis
      • hepatitis
      • portal hypertension
      • hypoalbuminaemia 2ndary to CCF, cirrhosis, CKD
  • cholelithiasis and choledocolithiasis
    • US imaging modality of choice followed by MRCP
    • CT only 30% sensitive depending on calcium content of calculi
    • Calculus appearance
      • echogenic mass with posterior shadowing
      • with or without biliary dilation
      • distal CBD may be difficult to visualise due to overlying duodenal gas - apply gentle probe pressure or roll into left lateral decubitus
    • Pitfalls
      • Hemobilia, Papillary tumours, Biliary sludge
      • These can obstruct CBD without causing acoustic shadowing

- thickened gallbladder wall in tranverse - long axis gallbaldder thickened wall - no gallstones → acalculous cholecystitis

- gallbaldder haemorrhage

Mirizzi Syndrome

  • =1 calculi within cystic duct or gallbladder causing extrahepatic biliary duct compression

    • results in inflammatory mass obstructing bile passage
  • Sonographic appearance
    • cystic duct calculous with surrounding oedema
    • dilated intrahepatic duct to level of common hepatic duct
    • may or may not have chronic cholecystitis

CBD strictures

  • unexplained structure of CBD or cystic duct should be viewed with caution
    • benign stictures - postsurgical or inflammatory conditions
    • malignancy - cholangiocarcinoma, pancreatic head adenocarcinoma (extrinsic masses), enalrged lymph nodes (extrinsic compression)

Gallbaldder perforation

  • traumatic perforation is rare
  • US - fluid collection adjacent to gallbladdder

- simple hepatic cyst - liver steatosis - biliary sludge - wall thickening > 3mm → cholecystitis
