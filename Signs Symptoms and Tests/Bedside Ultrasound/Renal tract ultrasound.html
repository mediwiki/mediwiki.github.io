Renal tract ultrasound

Method

  • supine position, may need R) or L) anterior oblique/lateral decubitus may be needed pending bowel gas
    • R) kidney should use liver as acoustic window

Anatomy

  • L) kidney - harder to visualise due to stomach and splenic flexure gas
    • L) subcostal can show lower pole
    • posterior lower intercostal level can see upper pole and mid-section
  • R) kidney - Morison's pouch is potential space,
    • central collecting system is echogenic
    • cortex is slightly less echogenic compared to liver
    • medullary pyramids are hypoechoic vs. surrounding cortex
  • Kidneys bean shaped
    • maximal longitudinal max length from upper to lower poles
    • transverse - round shaped, central echogenic collecting system
    • capsule is thin and hypoechoic
    • cortex is less echogenic than liver and spleen
    • medulalry pyramids are triangular and hypoechoic to cortex
      • anechoic when urine filled medullae
    • central sinus is echogenic with irregular borders
  • Measurements
    • Adults
      • 9-11cm length
      • 4-6cm transverse
      • 2.5-4cm depth
  • Normal anatomical variants
    • Dromedary bump - localised bulge of lateral border of kidney; (camel with one hump is a 'dromedary camel')
    • Hypertrophied column of Bertin - extension of renal cortical tissue separating pyramids
      • normal structures, but when hypertrophied can appear as a renal mass
    • duplex collecting system
      • bifid renal pelvus on US with/without 2 ureters
    • Horseshoe kidneys
      • lower poles are inferior and medially rotated w/ renal cortex fusion ANTERIOR to aorta and IVC
    • Renal ectopia
      • kidney located inferior to the normal position (e.g. pelvic kidney)
      • can cross midline to contralateral side and even fuse with the other kidney

- normal kidney - normal kidney

- Normal variant - dromedary bump - Column of Bertin - duplex collecting system - dilation of lower pole moiety related to vesicoureteric reflux - doppler can be used to show this is pelvis and NOT vein - horseshoe kidney anterior to aorta (hypoechoic region)

Renal pathology - Hydronephrosis

  • Causes
    • Renal calculi
    • Pelvis mass compression
    • bladder tumour
    • outflow obstruction
    • Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm
  • Degree of dilatation does NOT always correlate with degree of obstruction
  • sonographic hydronephrosis can appear as separation of renal pelvis/calycies due to dilatation
  • Grading
    • Mild - just evident calyx separation
    • Moderate- dilated calycies and pelvis w/o cortical compression
    • Severe - enalarged kidney, dilated calyx and pelvix, cortex compression
  • Localising obstruction in hydronephrosis
    • bilateral hydronephrosis → bladder, urethra
    • unilateral - PUJ, ureter, VUJ, Bladder
  • Parenchymal thickness - differentiate between acute and longstanding hydronephrosis
    • normal in acute obstruction
    • renal cortex atrophies with longstanding hydronephrosis

Renal pathology - Renal calculi

  • 3 areas of ureteric narrowing
    • PUJ
    • as it crosses iliac vessels
    • VUJ (75-80%)
  • Appear as echogenic foci with posterior acoustic shadowing
  • If diameter if < diameter of beam width, no posterior shadow is produced
    • colour doppler to test for 'twinkle artefact' produced by reflective rough surface

- calculi - twinkle artefact

Renal pathology - Acute pyelonephritis

  • majority of cases, kidney will appear normal
  • in some cases
    • diffuse or focal enlargement of renal cortex
    • lobar nephroma refers to localised renal swelling due to acute focal infection without liquefaction
    • cortex may show abnormal echotexture
      • hypoechoic from oedema
      • hyperechoic from haemorrhage
      • loss of corticomedulalry differentiation
    • colour doppler to show focal/diffuse ABSENCE of perfusion corresponding with oedema/inflammation

- lobar nephroma - acute pyelonephritis with abnormal echotexture - image left side, focal area of hypoperfusion

Renal pathology - Renal trauma

  • Lacerations
    • linear defects of cortex with variable echogenicity
  • subcapsular hematoma
    • perirenal fluid collection flattening underlying renal contour
  • perirenal hematoma
    • perirenal collection with does NOT alter renal contour
  • shattered kidney - multiple severe lacerations
    • multiple fragments of disorganised tissue with haemorrhage or renal collection
    • may have ureteropelvic avulsion, complete laceration/thrombus of the main renal artery or vein

- linear defects - subcapsular hematoma

Renal pathology - Renal cysts

  • Simple benign cysts
    • round or oval anechoic mass with posterior acoustic enhancement
    • wall of mass can be sharply defined or imperceptible
    • incidence in > 50 years → >27%
  • Complex renal cysts
    • internal echoes
    • septations
    • calcifications
    • perceptible defined wall
    • mural nodularity
    • May require further CT evaluation
