Female pelvis ultrasound

Approach

  • transabdominal and transvaginal
    • both can produce transverse and longitudinal uterus and ovary views

Anatomy

  • Uterus
    • Version - angle at cervix
      • Anteversion, retroversion
    • flexion - position of body of uterus
      • Anteflexion, retroflexion
    • most commonly anteverted anteflexed
  • Endometrium
    • premenopausal
      • thickness varies with menstrual cycle
        • <8mm in proliferative phase
        • <16mm in secretory phase
    • Postmenopausal

      • 5mm is abnormal

      • PV bleeding and > 5mm → >50% cancer

Uterine pathology

  • Hyperplasia - diffusely or focally thickened echogenic endometrium +/- small anechoic foci (demonstrating cystic change)
  • Endometrial polyps - focal endometrial thickening, sessile or pedunculated echogenic mass
    • intact ulverlying endometrium
    • on colour doppler - single stalk flow visible
  • Endometrial carcinoma
    • irregular endometrial thickening +/- mass
    • ill defined border between endometrium and myometrium
    • can invade myometrium and/or cervix
  • Adenomyosis - ectopic endometrial tissue in myometrium
    • diffuse uterine enlargement
    • loss of endometrial-myometrial interface
    • 'swiss cheese' appearance - hypoechoic myometrial cysts, hyperechoic endometrial glands
  • Uterine fibroids
    • benign smooth muscle neoplasm
    • found in submucosa, intralmural, or subserosal
    • hypo, iso or hyperechoic - usually hypoechoic
      • +/- calcification +/- cystic areas of necrosis or degeneration

- Endometrium - thickness measurement - endometrial hyperplasia with focally thickened endometrium - endometrial polyps - endometrial carcinoma w/ illdefined myometrial-endometrial border - adenomyosis

Fallopian tube pathology

  • Hydrosalpinx - fluid in fallopian tube
    • commonly from blockage of tube → buildup of serous fluids
    • wall of tube thin in acute cases, thickened in chronic cases
    • pyosalpinx - purulent fluid in tube
    • hematosalpinx - blood within tube

- hydrosalpinx

Ovarian pathology

  • Polycystic ovaries
    • bilateral enlargement >10cc

    • 12 peripheral 2-9mm follicles

    • 'string of pearls' appearance
    • central stromal brightness
    • describes increased number of follicles in ovary on imaging
    • PCOS metabolic disorder has classical triad of oligomenorhoea, hirsutism, obesity
      • Rotterdam consensus - oligo/anovulation, hyperandrogenism, polycystic ovaries on imaging - 2 of 3 are needed
  • Ovarian torsion
    • enlarged ovary with little/no venous flow +/- arterial flow
    • hypo or hyperechoic ovary
    • if intermittent torsion, US doppler flows may be normal - need to exclude underlying ovarian lesion
  • Functional Cysts AKA follicular cysts
    • common - occur when ovum not released or follicle doesn't dissolve after ovulation;
    • unilocular anechoic structures with smooth thin walls - should be <5cm and tend to be self-resolving
  • Corpus luteal cysts
    • hypervascular margin - 'ring of fire' appearance on doppler US
  • Endometriomas
    • localised collection of endometriosis within ovary
    • generally 2-5cm, up to 20cm
    • due to haemorrhagic debris - present as homogenous low-level internal echos ('ground glass echos with acoustic enhancement')
  • Tumours
    • Epithelial cell tumours - 60-70% - most common
      • benign OR malignant; cystic OR solid
      • increased likelihood of malignancy if: solid tissue elements, thicker septae, greater vasculaarity
    • Germ cell tumours - 15-20% - 60^ in children and adolescents
      • Dermoid cyst/teratoma
        • most common benign tumour
        • cystic mass with mural components
        • cystic w/ dense echogenic nodule projecting into cyst lumen - 'Rokitansky nodule' - will likely be source of hair in cyst
        • Teeth or bone likely will likely be WITHIN nodule
        • hair or sebaceous material can be diffusely or partially echogenic → posterior sound attenuation, resulting in only the tip of cyst visible → 'tip of the iceberg sign'
        • on CT - fat attenuation within cyst is diagnostic
    • Stromal tumours - <10%

- PCOS - ovarian torsion - follicular cyst - corpus luteal cyst - ring of fire - endometrioma cyst - epithelial cell tumour - dermoid cyst / teratoma
