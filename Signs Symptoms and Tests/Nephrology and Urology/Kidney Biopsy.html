Kidney Biopsy

Indications

  • glomerular hematuria
  • severely increased albuminuria
  • AKI or CKD of unknown cause
  • kidney transplant dysfunction or monitoring
  • AIN and atheroembolic disease

Contraindications for percutaneous biopsy

  • uncooperative patient
  • bleeding diatheses (including antiplatelet use or anticoagulation)
  • uncontrolled hypertension
  • poor kidney visualisation
  • atrophic kidneys
  • active UTI

Relative contraindications for percutaneous biopsy

  • Pregnancy
  • Solitary kidney

Risks of kidney biopsy

  • retroperitoneal bleeding
    • major complications (e.g transfusion, angiography +- embolisation, hemodynamic instability) < 3%
    • Assess with CT + contrast
  • Pain and hematuria - minor, more frequent
  • Kidney loss and death are very rare
